# Fundraising Deck

## Overview
This document provides a comprehensive slide-by-slide outline for your investor pitch deck, along with supporting financial frameworks for valuation, unit economics, and cap table planning.

---

## Pitch Deck Outline (10-15 Slides)

### Slide 1: Cover
- **Company Name & Logo**
- **Tagline**: [PLACEHOLDER: One sentence describing what you do]
- **Contact Info**: Founder name, email, date
- **Confidential Notice**

### Slide 2: Problem
- **The Pain Point**: [PLACEHOLDER: Describe the problem your target customers face]
- **Market Evidence**: Statistics, quotes, or pain points from real users
- **Why Now**: Why this problem is urgent or growing
- **Visual**: Chart or image illustrating the problem

### Slide 3: Solution
- **Your Product/Service**: [PLACEHOLDER: Describe your solution in 1-2 sentences]
- **Key Features**: 3-4 bullet points on what makes it work
- **How It Works**: Simple diagram or user flow
- **Why It's Better**: Differentiation from existing alternatives

### Slide 4: Product Demo
- **Screenshots/Video**: [PLACEHOLDER: Include actual product images or demo video link]
- **User Journey**: Show how a customer uses your product
- **Key Value Props**: Highlight what users love most
- **Traction Proof**: Early user testimonials or metrics

### Slide 5: Market Opportunity
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: [PLACEHOLDER: $X billion]
- **SAM (Serviceable Available Market)**: [PLACEHOLDER: $X billion]
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: [PLACEHOLDER: $X million in 3 years]
- **Market Growth Rate**: X% CAGR
- **Source Citations**: Link to market research reports

### Slide 6: Business Model
- **Revenue Streams**: [PLACEHOLDER: Subscription, transaction fees, etc.]
- **Pricing Strategy**: See detailed section below
- **Unit Economics**: CAC, LTV, payback period (see section below)
- **Path to Profitability**: When you expect to break even

### Slide 7: Traction
- **Key Metrics**: [PLACEHOLDER: Revenue, users, MRR/ARR, growth rate]
- **Growth Chart**: Visual showing month-over-month or year-over-year growth
- **Customer Logos**: If you have notable customers
- **Milestones Achieved**: Product launch, partnerships, revenue milestones

### Slide 8: Go-to-Market Strategy
- **Target Customer Segments**: [PLACEHOLDER: Define ICP]
- **Acquisition Channels**: SEO, paid ads, partnerships, sales team
- **Customer Journey**: Awareness → Consideration → Purchase → Retention
- **Competitive Advantages**: Why you'll win in the market

### Slide 9: Competition
- **Competitive Landscape**: 2x2 matrix or table
- **Direct Competitors**: [PLACEHOLDER: List 3-5 competitors]
- **Indirect Competitors/Alternatives**: Current solutions customers use
- **Your Differentiation**: Why you're better (speed, cost, UX, technology)

### Slide 10: Team
- **Founders & Key Team Members**: [PLACEHOLDER: Names, titles, headshots]
- **Relevant Experience**: Previous companies, domain expertise, technical skills
- **Advisors/Board Members**: Notable names that add credibility
- **Why This Team**: Why you're uniquely positioned to solve this problem

### Slide 11: Financials
- **Historical Performance**: Last 12-24 months if available
- **3-Year Projections**: Revenue, gross margin, EBITDA (see detailed model below)
- **Key Assumptions**: Growth rate, CAC, LTV, pricing
- **Path to Profitability**: When you'll be cash flow positive

### Slide 12: The Ask
- **Funding Amount**: [PLACEHOLDER: Raising $X million]
- **Use of Funds**: Breakdown by category (product 40%, sales/marketing 35%, hiring 20%, operations 5%)
- **Milestones**: What you'll achieve with this capital
- **Runway**: How many months this funding provides

### Slide 13: Vision
- **Long-Term Vision**: [PLACEHOLDER: Where you see the company in 5-10 years]
- **Future Product Roadmap**: What comes after current product
- **Market Expansion**: Geographic or vertical expansion plans
- **Exit Potential**: Strategic acquirers or IPO path

### Slide 14: Appendix (Optional)
- Detailed financials
- Additional customer testimonials
- Technical architecture
- Intellectual property
- Press coverage

- [ ] Payback period < 18 months
- [ ] Gross margin > 70% (SaaS) or industry benchmark
- [ ] Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) growth > 15% MoM
- [ ] Net revenue retention > 100% (with expansion)
- [ ] Clear path to $1M ARR (Seed) or $10M ARR (Series A)
- [ ] Product-market fit signals (NPS > 50, organic growth, low churn)
