## Slide 4: Market Opportunity

**Purpose:** Prove the market is large enough to build a big business

**Content:**
- **Market Size Framework (TAM/SAM/SOM)**
  - **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** [PLACEHOLDER: $X billion]
    - The total market demand for your product category
  - **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):** [PLACEHOLDER: $X billion]
    - The segment of TAM you can reach with your business model
  - **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** [PLACEHOLDER: $X million]
    - The portion you can realistically capture in 3-5 years

- **Market Growth**
  - [PLACEHOLDER: Growth rate and trends]
  - [PLACEHOLDER: Key drivers of growth]

- **Market Validation**
  - [PLACEHOLDER: Industry reports, analyst predictions, or market signals]

**Best Practices:**
- Use credible sources (Gartner, IDC, etc.)
- Show bottom-up calculations, not just top-down
- Demonstrate you understand your market deeply

## Pitch Deck Best Practices

### General Guidelines
- **Length:** 10-15 slides for the main deck (exclude appendix)
- **Time:** Should support a 15-20 minute presentation
- **Design:** Clean, consistent, professional
- **Font:** Large enough to read (minimum 24pt for body text)
- **One Message Per Slide:** Each slide should have one clear takeaway

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Too much text (use visuals and bullet points)
- Overly technical jargon
- Unrealistic projections
- Ignoring or dismissing competition
- Weak team slide
- Unclear ask
- Poor design quality

### Tailoring for Audience
- **Angel Investors:** Focus on vision and founding team
- **VCs:** Emphasize market size, traction, and scalability
- **Strategic Investors:** Highlight partnership potential and synergies

### Preparation Tips
- **Know Your Numbers:** Be ready to defend every metric
- **Practice Your Delivery:** Rehearse 10+ times
- **Anticipate Questions:** Prepare answers for tough questions
- **Tell a Story:** Connect the slides into a narrative arc
- **Time Management:** Stay within time limits
