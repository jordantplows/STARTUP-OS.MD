## The LAER Framework

### Objection Handling Methodology

Never respond to objections defensively. Use LAER to understand the root cause and address it thoughtfully.

**L - Listen:**
- Let the customer fully articulate their concern
- Don't interrupt or jump to defend
- Take notes on specific language they use

**A - Acknowledge:**
- Validate their concern (don't dismiss it)
- Show empathy: "I understand why that's a concern..."
- Build trust by acknowledging the legitimacy of the objection

**E - Explore:**
- Ask clarifying questions to understand the root cause
- "Tell me more about that..."
- "What specifically concerns you about [X]?"
- Often the stated objection is not the real objection

**R - Respond:**
- Address the root cause, not just the surface objection
- Use proof points: Data, customer stories, case studies
- Offer options or alternatives where possible
- Check if you've resolved the concern: "Does that address your concern?"

### Objection Handling Principles

1. **Welcome Objections:** They're buying signals (they're engaged enough to push back)
2. **Never Argue:** You can win the argument and lose the deal
3. **Isolate the Objection:** "If we can resolve [X], is there anything else holding you back?"
4. **Proof Over Claims:** Show, don't tell (use data, customer stories, demos)
5. **Offer Alternatives:** Give them a path forward that addresses their concern

## Pricing Objections

### Objection 1: "It's too expensive"

**LAER Response:**

**Listen:** [Let them explain why they think it's expensive]

**Acknowledge:**
```
"I appreciate you being direct about cost. That's an important consideration."
```

**Explore:**
```
"Help me understand - when you say 'too expensive,' what are you comparing it to?
- Is it outside your budget range?
- Does it not align with the value you'd get?
- Or is it more than you expected based on [competitor / alternative]?"
```

**Respond:**

**Option A: If it's about budget:**
```
"I understand. Let's look at this from a value perspective. Based on our discovery, you're currently spending [X hours/week] on [manual process] and losing [Y deals/revenue] due to [pain point].

The cost of the problem:
- Time cost: [X hours] × [people] × [rate] = $[A]/month
- Opportunity cost: [Lost deals/revenue] = $[B]/quarter
- Total current state cost: $[C]/year

Our solution costs $[D]/year, which means you're net positive $[E]/year. So the real question is: Can you afford NOT to solve this?

[Pause]

Would it help to break this into smaller steps? We could start with a pilot for [smaller team/shorter term] to prove the ROI, then expand."
```

**Option B: If it's about value:**
```
"Let me make sure we're aligned on the value. Based on what you shared, we'd help you:
- [Outcome 1]: Save [X hours/week] = $[Y]/year
- [Outcome 2]: Improve [metric] by [Z]% = $[W]/year
- [Outcome 3]: [Other impact] = $[V]/year

Total value: $[Total]/year
Investment: $[Price]/year
ROI: [X]X return, [Y] month payback

If we're not delivering that value, then yes, it's too expensive. But if we do, it's actually underpriced.

What would you need to see to feel confident in that ROI?"
```

**Option C: If it's competitive pricing:**
```
"I hear you're comparing us to [Competitor at lower price]. Let's look at total cost of ownership:

Our price: $[X]/year
Includes: [List key features/services]
Time to value: [Y] days
Expected ROI: [Z]X

Competitor price: $[W]/year
Excludes: [Premium features/services they'd need to pay extra for]
Implementation: [Longer time, more services needed]
Hidden costs: [Integrations, add-ons, support]

When you add it all up, our 3-year TCO is actually [X%] lower, and you get [key differentiator].

[Customer story]
[Company X] initially went with [cheaper competitor] to save money. After 6 months, they switched to us because [specific reason]. Their quote: '[PLACEHOLDER]'

Would it help to see a side-by-side TCO comparison?"
```

**Proof Points:**
- ROI calculator with their data
- TCO comparison vs. competitors
- Customer case study with similar ROI
- Reference call with customer who had same concern

### Objection 2: "We don't have budget"

**LAER Response:**

**Listen:** [Let them explain budget situation]

**Acknowledge:**
```
"I understand. Budget constraints are real, especially in [current economic environment / their situation]."
```

**Explore:**
```
"Help me understand the budget situation:
- Is there no budget allocated for this type of solution this year?
- Or is the budget allocated elsewhere right now?
- When does your next budget cycle start?
- What would it take to get budget allocated or reallocated?"
```

**Respond:**

**Option A: If budget exists but needs reallocation:**
```
"Got it. Let me ask: You mentioned you're currently spending $[X] on [tools you'd replace] and [Y hours] on [manual process]. That's effectively $[Z] in budget already allocated to solving this problem, just inefficiently.

What if we could reallocate that existing spend to a better solution? You're not asking for new budget - you're optimizing what you're already spending.

Would it help if we showed how this could be budget-neutral or even cost-saving?"
```

**Option B: If budget cycle is coming:**
```
"I appreciate that. When does your next budget cycle start? [Wait for answer]

Here's what I'd suggest: Let's get you set up with a trial or pilot now so you can prove the value, and then you have the data you need to secure budget for [next quarter/year].

[Customer story]
[Company X] did exactly this - ran a 30-day pilot with no commitment, demonstrated [ROI], and got budget approved for full rollout the following quarter. Would that work for you?"
```

**Option C: If budget is truly unavailable:**
```
"I understand. Let me ask: What's the cost of not solving this problem while you wait for budget?

Based on what you've shared, waiting [6 months] costs you:
- [X hours/week] of wasted time = $[A]
- [Y deals] lost or delayed = $[B]
- Total cost of waiting: $[C]

Compare that to our annual cost of $[D], and you're actually losing money by waiting.

[Pause]

Would it make sense to explore flexible payment terms? We offer:
- Monthly payments instead of annual prepay
- Pilot/trial with no upfront cost to prove ROI
- Start date flexibility - sign now, start when budget is available

What would make this work within your constraints?"
```

**Proof Points:**
- Cost of inaction analysis
- Budget reallocation analysis (tools you replace)
- Customer story: Got budget approved after trial
- Flexible payment terms options

**LAER Response:**

**Listen:** [Let them share competitor pricing]

**Acknowledge:**
```
"Thanks for being transparent. I appreciate you're evaluating multiple options and being mindful of cost."
```

**Explore:**
```
"Help me understand what you're comparing:
- What tier/plan are you looking at with [Competitor]?
- What features are included at that price point?
- Have they quoted implementation and support costs?
- How does their solution compare on [key differentiator]?"
```

**Respond:**

**See `/04-finance/pricing-strategy.md` for competitor-specific pricing comparisons.**

**Option A: Show TCO difference:**
```
"You're right that [Competitor]'s list price is lower. But let's compare total cost of ownership:

[Show spreadsheet or slide]

Competitor X:
- Base price: $[W]/year
- Implementation services: $[X] (they require professional services)
- Add-ons needed: $[Y]/year (features we include in base tier)
- Hidden costs: [Integration fees, premium support, etc.]
- Total Year 1: $[Z]

Our solution:
- Base price: $[A]/year (includes implementation, all features, premium support)
- Total Year 1: $[A]

Difference: We're actually [X%] less expensive over 3 years.

Plus, our customers see [faster time-to-value / better ROI / higher adoption]. What good is a cheaper solution if it takes twice as long to implement and delivers half the value?"
```

**Option B: Differentiate on value:**
```
"I hear you on price. Let me ask: If both solutions cost the same, which would you choose? [Wait for answer]

If you'd choose us, then the question becomes: Is the value gap worth the price difference?

Here's what you get with us that you don't get with [Competitor]:
- [Key Differentiator #1 + impact]
- [Key Differentiator #2 + impact]
- [Key Differentiator #3 + impact]

[Customer story]
[Company X] chose us over [Competitor] even though we were [X%] more expensive because [specific reason]. Six months later, they've seen [specific ROI] and told us it was an easy decision in hindsight.

Would it help to quantify the value of those differentiators for your specific situation?"
```

**Option C: Offer price match consideration (if appropriate per pricing policy):**
```
"I appreciate you sharing that. While I can't always match competitor pricing, let me explore what we can do.

If [Competitor] is at $[X] and we're at $[Y], that's a $[Z] difference. Let me show you what you'd give up if we came down to match them:
- [Feature/service A] (valued at $[X])
- [Feature/service B] (valued at $[Y])

Alternatively, we could look at:
- Multi-year commitment for [X%] discount
- Start with smaller team/pilot to reduce initial cost
- Flexible payment terms to ease cash flow

What's more important to you: Lowest price, or best value?"
```

**Proof Points:**
- TCO comparison spreadsheet
- Competitive battlecard (see `sales-playbook.md`)
- Customer switching story (Competitor → Us)
- Feature comparison showing gaps in competitor

### Objection 4: "Can you give us a discount?"

**LAER Response:**

**Listen:** [Let them explain why they're asking]

**Acknowledge:**
```
"I appreciate you asking. Most of our customers negotiate on pricing - it's a normal part of the process."
```

**Explore:**
```
"Help me understand:
- Is price the only thing standing between us and moving forward?
- What would make this a no-brainer investment for you?
- Are you comparing our price to a specific alternative or budget threshold?"
```

**Respond:**

**See `/04-finance/pricing-strategy.md` for approved discount policy.**

**Option A: Anchor to value, offer standard discounts:**
```
"Our pricing reflects the [ROI/value] we deliver. That said, we do have standard discounts for certain commitments:

- Annual prepayment: 15% discount (instead of monthly)
- Multi-year: 20% discount for 2-year, 25% for 3-year
- Volume: [X%] discount at [Y] users

[Pause]

Would any of those work for you? For example, if you can commit to annual prepayment, I can bring the price from $[X]/month to $[Y]/year, which saves you $[Z]."
```

**Option B: Trade discount for value:**
```
"I'd like to help with pricing. Let me ask: What would you be willing to give in return?

Here are things that are valuable to us:
- Multi-year commitment (we offer [X%] discount)
- Case study and public reference (we offer [Y%] discount)
- Prepayment (we offer [Z%] discount)
- Larger initial deployment (volume discount)

What would work for you?"
```

**Option C: Offer value-adds instead of discount:**
```
"Instead of discounting, what if we added value? I could offer:
- Free implementation services (normally $[X])
- Extended onboarding and training ($[Y] value)
- Upgrade to [premium tier features] at [current tier price]
- Dedicated success manager for first 90 days ($[Z] value)

That's $[Total] in added value without reducing price. Would that work?"
```

**Option D: Use scarcity/urgency (if legitimate):**
```
"Let me see what I can do. If we can sign by [end of quarter/month], I can offer [X%] discount. But I'd need your commitment that we're moving forward. Does that work?"
```

**Proof Points:**
- Pricing justification: ROI analysis showing value
- Discount policy: Clear trade-offs for discount
- Customer story: "They didn't need a discount once they saw the value"

## Timing Objections

### Objection 5: "We're not ready to buy right now"

**LAER Response:**

**Listen:** [Let them explain why timing is off]

**Acknowledge:**
```
"I appreciate you being upfront about timing. I'd rather know that now than waste your time or mine."
```

**Explore:**
```
"Help me understand:
- What needs to happen before you're ready?
- Is this a timing issue or is there something else holding you back?
- When do you think you'll be ready?
- What's the risk of waiting [X months] to solve this problem?"
```

**Respond:**

**Option A: Create urgency by quantifying cost of delay:**
```
"I hear you on timing. Let me ask: What's the cost of waiting?

Based on what you've shared, every month you wait costs you:
- [X hours] of wasted time = $[Y]
- [Z] in lost revenue/opportunity = $[W]
- Total cost of delay: $[Total]/month

If you wait [3-6 months], that's $[Total x months] in value left on the table.

Compare that to the cost of getting started now: $[Price/month]. You're actually losing money by waiting.

What would need to change to move this up in priority?"
```

**Option B: Reduce barrier to entry:**
```
"I understand. What if we made it easier to get started? We could:
- Run a pilot with [smaller team] to minimize disruption
- Start implementation but delay go-live to [when you're ready]
- Sign contract now, flex start date to [future date]

That way you're not losing ground while you wait, and when you're ready, you're already set up. Would that work?"
```

**Option C: Stay engaged with nurture plan:**
```
"Totally understand. Here's what I'd suggest:

Let's stay in touch over the next [timeframe]. I'll:
- Check in monthly to see if timing has changed
- Share relevant content and updates
- Keep you on our roadmap and product update list

In the meantime, is there anything we can do to help you prepare? For example:
- Run a free assessment of your current process
- Provide a trial so you can kick the tires
- Connect you with a customer in your industry

When would be a good time to revisit this?"
```

**Proof Points:**
- Cost of delay calculation
- Customer story: "Wished they'd started sooner"
- Pilot/trial option to reduce risk

### Objection 6: "We need more time to evaluate"

**LAER Response:**

**Listen:** [Let them explain what more time accomplishes]

**Acknowledge:**
```
"I appreciate that you want to be thorough. This is an important decision."
```

**Explore:**
```
"Help me understand what you need more time for:
- Is there more information you need from us?
- Are there other stakeholders who need to evaluate?
- Are you waiting to compare other vendors?
- Is there a specific decision process or timeline you're following?

What would give you confidence to make a decision sooner?"
```

**Respond:**

**Option A: Provide what's missing:**
```
"Got it. It sounds like you need [information/proof/validation]. Let me help with that:

- [If technical validation needed]: Let's set up a technical deep-dive with your team
- [If reference needed]: Let me connect you with [similar customer]
- [If ROI validation needed]: Let me build you a detailed business case
- [If stakeholder buy-in needed]: Let's get [economic buyer] involved in next conversation

What specifically would you need to see to feel comfortable moving forward?"
```

**Option B: Isolate the objection:**
```
"Fair enough. Let me ask: If we gave you more time, what would change?

[Listen]

Is there anything other than time standing between us and moving forward? [Listen]

If time is the only issue, here's what I'd suggest: Let's move forward with a pilot or trial while you're evaluating. That way:
- You're not losing time - you're learning with real data
- You can make a more informed decision based on actual results, not just demos
- If it doesn't work out, you haven't committed long-term

Would a [30-day trial] give you the confidence you need?"
```

**Option C: Create urgency:**
```
"I understand. The challenge is that every day you wait, you're continuing to experience [pain point] and it's costing you $[X]/month.

Additionally, if we can get started by [date], we can have you live by [date] and delivering value by [date]. If we wait, you're pushing that timeline out by [X weeks/months].

What's the least risky way to move forward without needing more time? Could we start small and expand once you're confident?"
```

**Proof Points:**
- Clear timeline: "Here's exactly what happens after you sign"
- Trial/pilot option: "Test it before you commit"
- Customer story: "They moved quickly and didn't regret it"

### Objection 10: "We're going with [Competitor]"

**LAER Response:**

**Listen:** [Let them explain their decision]

**Acknowledge:**
```
"I appreciate you letting me know. Congratulations on making a decision - I know this wasn't easy."
```

**Explore:**
```
"Before you finalize, would you mind sharing:
- What made [Competitor] the better choice?
- Was there anything we could have done differently?
- Is the contract signed, or are you still in negotiation?
- On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you in the decision?"
```

**Respond:**

**Option A: If contract not yet signed (last-ditch effort):**
```
"I appreciate the feedback. Before you sign, let me ask: If I could address [concern they mentioned], would that change your decision?

[Listen]

Here's what I'd like to offer:
- [Solution to their stated reason for choosing competitor]
- [Better pricing / terms if that was a factor]
- [Reference call with customer who switched from Competitor]

We really want to earn your business, and I believe we're the better fit for [specific reason]. Would you give me one more shot to show you why?"
```

**Option B: If decision is final, stay on good terms:**
```
"I understand. While I'm disappointed we didn't win your business, I respect your decision.

A couple of things:
1. If [Competitor] doesn't work out or you run into issues, please reach out. We'd love to be your backup plan.
2. Many of our best customers initially chose [Competitor] and came back to us later. We'll be here when you're ready.
3. Can I stay in touch? I'd love to send you [industry insights / product updates / helpful content] even if you're not a customer.

Also, any chance you'd be willing to do a 15-minute win/loss debrief call so I can learn from this? Your feedback would be really valuable.

Best of luck with the implementation, and thanks for your time."
```

**Option C: Plant seed for future switch:**
```
"Got it. Congrats again on moving forward.

One thing I'd encourage: Keep us in mind as a benchmark. Many customers evaluate us 6-12 months after implementing [Competitor] because [common issue they run into].

Would it be okay if I checked in with you in [6 months] to see how things are going? I promise not to be pushy - just genuinely curious to hear how [Competitor] is working for you.

[If they agree]
Great, I'll put a reminder to reach out in [month]. In the meantime, if you need anything, I'm here."
```
