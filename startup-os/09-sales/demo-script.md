## Overview

This demo script provides a problem-first demonstration framework that connects our solution to specific customer pain points identified during discovery. The goal is to tell a compelling story that shows how we solve their problems, not to present a feature tour.

## Demo Principles

### Problem-First, Not Feature-First

**Don't do this (Feature Tour):**
"Let me show you our dashboard. Here's the navigation, here are filters, here's how you export data..."

**Do this (Problem-First):**
"You mentioned you spend 10 hours a week manually building forecasts. Let me show you how we eliminate that. Imagine it's Monday morning and you need to present forecast to your CRO..."

### Core Principles

1. **Start with Their Pain:** Begin each section by restating the problem they shared in discovery
2. **Show, Don't Tell:** Demonstrate outcomes, not features ("This cuts forecast time by 80%" vs. "Here's a forecast feature")
3. **Use Their Data:** Whenever possible, demo with their terminology, metrics, and examples
4. **Tell a Story:** Walk through a real-world scenario they'll recognize
5. **Pause for Validation:** Check understanding frequently ("Does this solve what you described?")
6. **Focus on Top 3 Pains:** Don't show everything - show what matters most
7. **Differentiate:** Highlight what competitors can't do (not just what we can do)
8. **Prove It:** Use data, customer stories, and proof points throughout

## Demo Structure Overview

### Recommended Flow (45-60 Minutes)

| Section | Time | Purpose |
|---------|------|---------|
| **1. Opening & Discovery Recap** | 5 min | Set agenda, confirm pain points |
| **2. Pain #1 Demo (Highest Priority)** | 15 min | Deep dive on most critical pain |
| **3. Pain #2 Demo (Differentiator)** | 10 min | Show competitive advantage |
| **4. Pain #3 Demo (Quick Win)** | 5 min | Address remaining concern |
| **5. Integration & Implementation** | 5 min | Address "how does this work?" questions |
| **6. Social Proof & ROI** | 5 min | Customer stories and expected value |
| **7. Objection Handling** | 5-10 min | Proactively address concerns |
| **8. Next Steps & Close** | 5 min | Confirm fit, propose next steps |

**Flexibility:** Adjust based on customer engagement and time constraints. Always prioritize Pain #1.

## Section 1: Opening & Discovery Recap (5 Minutes)

### Objectives

- Build rapport and set expectations
- Confirm pain points and priorities
- Get buy-in on agenda
- Clarify attendee roles

### Script

**Welcome & Rapport:**
```
"Hi [Names], thanks for making time today. Great to see you again, [Champion Name], and welcome [New Attendees]."

[If new attendees] "Let me do quick introductions. I'm [Your Name], [Your Role]. With me is [SE Name], [SE Role]. Can everyone quickly introduce themselves and share what you're hoping to see today?"
```

**Set Agenda:**
```
"Here's what I'd like to cover in the next [45-60] minutes:

1. Quick recap of what we discussed in discovery - the challenges you're facing
2. Show you exactly how we solve those specific problems
3. Address any questions or concerns
4. Talk about next steps if this looks like a good fit

Sound good?"
```

**Recap Discovery:**
```
"When we talked last week, you shared three main challenges:

1. [Pain Point #1 - e.g., 'You're spending 10+ hours a week manually building forecasts, and they're still not accurate']
2. [Pain Point #2 - e.g., 'You can't see which deals are at risk until it's too late to save them']
3. [Pain Point #3 - e.g., 'New reps take 6 months to ramp, costing you quota capacity']

Did I get that right? Anything else you'd like me to cover today?"
```

**Confirm Priorities:**
```
"Of those three, which is most critical to solve? [Wait for response]

Great, I'll start with that one and go deep, then we'll cover the others. I'll pause frequently for questions, so jump in anytime."
```

## Section 2: Pain #1 Demo (15 Minutes)

### Structure for Each Pain Point

1. **Restate the Problem** (1 min) - Echo back their pain in their words
2. **Show Current State** (2 min) - Acknowledge how they do it today (manual, competitor)
3. **Introduce Our Solution** (5 min) - Demo the feature/workflow that solves it
4. **Quantify the Impact** (2 min) - Show time saved, accuracy improved, etc.
5. **Check Understanding** (1 min) - "Does this solve what you described?"

---

## Demo Do's and Don'ts

### Do:

- **Customize:** Tailor demo to their pain points (not a generic feature tour)
- **Tell a Story:** "Imagine it's Monday morning..." (not "Here's a feature")
- **Use Their Data:** Their industry, metrics, terminology
- **Pause Frequently:** Check understanding, invite questions
- **Show Outcomes:** "This saves 8 hours/week" (not "Here's how the button works")
- **Differentiate:** Highlight what competitors can't do
- **Quantify Value:** Time saved, revenue impact, ROI
- **End with Action:** Clear next steps, book meeting before ending

### Don't:

- **Feature Dump:** Don't show everything - show what matters
- **Talk Too Much:** 60/40 rule - they should talk 40% of the time
- **Go Too Deep Too Soon:** Start high-level, drill down only if asked
- **Ignore Questions:** If they ask a question, stop and answer
- **Wing It:** Always prepare and customize based on discovery
- **Trash Competitors:** Stay professional, focus on differentiation
- **Forget to Close:** Always end with clear next steps
- **Let Tech Fail You:** Test beforehand, have backup plan
