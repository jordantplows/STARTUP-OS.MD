## SPIN Selling Framework

### The SPIN Model

SPIN is a question-based selling methodology developed by Neil Rackham. The acronym represents four types of questions asked in sequence:

1. **Situation Questions** - Understand current state, context, and background
2. **Problem Questions** - Uncover difficulties, dissatisfaction, and pain points
3. **Implication Questions** - Amplify the consequences and cost of problems
4. **Need-Payoff Questions** - Co-create the vision of success and value

### Question Sequence Strategy

**Early Discovery (First 15 Minutes):**
- Heavy on Situation questions (build context)
- Introduce Problem questions (identify pain points)
- Goal: Understand the landscape and surface initial pain

**Mid Discovery (15-30 Minutes):**
- Deep Problem questions (explore each pain point)
- Introduce Implication questions (quantify impact)
- Goal: Prioritize pain points and understand business impact

**Late Discovery (30-45 Minutes):**
- Implication questions (amplify cost of inaction)
- Need-Payoff questions (co-create the vision)
- Goal: Align on desired outcomes and success criteria

### Best Practices

**Do:**
- Ask open-ended questions ("Tell me about..." vs. "Do you...?")
- Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule)
- Pause after questions to let customer think
- Follow up with "Tell me more" and "Why is that important?"
- Take detailed notes for proposal and demo customization

**Don't:**
- Pitch or present features during discovery
- Ask leading questions that telegraph the answer you want
- Skip Situation questions (you'll miss critical context)
- Jump to solution before pain is fully quantified
- Interrogate (make it a conversation, not an interview)

## Situation Questions

### Purpose

Understand the customer's current state, processes, tools, team structure, and context. These questions establish credibility and provide the foundation for Problem questions.

### Guidelines

- Keep concise (10-15 minutes max)
- Pre-research as much as possible (don't ask what you can find on LinkedIn)
- Focus on areas relevant to your solution
- Transition to Problem questions once you understand the landscape

---

## Situation Questions by Topic

### Current Process & Workflow

**Objective:** Understand how they currently accomplish the job-to-be-done

**Questions:**

1. "Walk me through your current process for [PLACEHOLDER - forecasting / pipeline management / reporting]."
   - **Follow-up:** "How often do you do this? Daily, weekly, monthly?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How much time does this typically take?"

2. "Who's involved in [process]? What are their roles?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How do they collaborate? Where does information get handed off?"

3. "What does a typical [day / week / quarter] look like for you in terms of [relevant workflow]?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What takes up most of your time?"

4. "Take me through a recent example where you needed to [accomplish specific task]. What did that look like?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What went well? What was challenging?"

5. "How has this process evolved over time?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What worked at smaller scale that doesn't work now?"

---

### Tools & Technology Stack

**Objective:** Understand current tools, integrations, and technical environment

**Questions:**

1. "What tools do you currently use for [PLACEHOLDER - CRM / analytics / automation]?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How long have you been using them?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What drove the decision to use those tools?"

2. "How do [Tool A] and [Tool B] work together?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Is data synced automatically or manually?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Where do data inconsistencies show up?"

3. "Walk me through where your data lives. Where does it originate? Where does it flow?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Who's responsible for maintaining data quality?"

4. "What tools have you evaluated or tried in the past that didn't work out?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What went wrong? What would need to be different?"

5. "Are there any technical constraints or requirements we should know about?"
   - **Example prompts:** Security, compliance, integrations, on-prem vs. cloud

---

### Team Structure & Stakeholders

**Objective:** Map the org chart, understand roles, and identify decision-makers

**Questions:**

1. "Tell me about your team structure. Who reports to you? Who do you report to?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How many people are in your organization / department?"

2. "Who else is impacted by [problem / process / workflow]?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How does this affect [Sales / Marketing / CS / Finance]?"

3. "When you've purchased similar software in the past, who was involved in the decision?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What was the evaluation process?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Who had final say?"

4. "Who are the key stakeholders we'd need to get aligned on a solution like this?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What does [CRO / VP Sales / CFO] care most about?"

5. "If we move forward, who would be responsible for implementation and adoption?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Do you have internal resources (IT, RevOps) or would you need vendor support?"

---

### Goals & Success Metrics

**Objective:** Understand what they're measured on and what success looks like

**Questions:**

1. "What are your top priorities this [quarter / year]?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How is your performance measured?"

2. "What metrics are you personally responsible for?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Where are you today vs. target?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would it take to close that gap?"

3. "What does success look like for you 6 months from now?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would make this year a win for your team?"

4. "What strategic initiatives is the company focused on?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How does your team contribute to those?"

5. "If we're having this conversation a year from now and you're thrilled with the results, what happened?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What changed? What metrics improved?"

---

### Budget & Timeline Context

**Objective:** Understand budget availability, approval process, and timeline

**Questions:**

1. "Do you have budget allocated for this type of solution?"
   - **If yes:** "What's the budget range you're working within?"
   - **If no:** "What would the process look like to secure budget?"

2. "When do you need to have a solution in place?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's driving that timeline?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What happens if that deadline isn't met?"

3. "Have you purchased similar software before? What was that process like?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How long did it take from first conversation to signed contract?"

4. "Are there any upcoming events that would influence timing?"
   - **Example prompts:** Budget cycles, quarter-end, new hire onboarding, product launch

## Problem Questions

### Purpose

Uncover difficulties, dissatisfaction, and pain points with the current state. These questions help the customer articulate problems they may not have fully recognized.

### Guidelines

- Ask about problems, not solutions (avoid "Would you like a feature that...?")
- Focus on pain points your product solves (don't go down rabbit holes)
- Use silence after asking - let customer think and elaborate
- Probe each pain point before moving to next

## Problem Questions by Persona

### For Sales Leaders (VP/Director of Sales)

**Top Pain Points:** Manual forecasting, poor pipeline visibility, team productivity, ramp time

**Questions:**

1. "You mentioned you spend [X hours] on forecasting each week. What makes that process so time-consuming?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would happen if you could cut that time in half?"

2. "How confident are you in your forecast accuracy today?"
   - **Follow-up:** "When was the last time your forecast was significantly off? What happened?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How does forecast inaccuracy impact your relationship with the CRO or board?"

3. "Walk me through how you currently get visibility into your pipeline health."
   - **Follow-up:** "What signals do you wish you could see earlier?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Where do deals fall through the cracks?"

4. "How do you know which deals are on track vs. at risk?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What does it take to figure that out today?"
   - **Follow-up:** "By the time you realize a deal is at risk, is it too late to save it?"

5. "Tell me about your process for coaching your team on deals."
   - **Follow-up:** "How much time do you spend coaching vs. administrative work?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Where do you wish you could spend more time?"

6. "How long does it take new reps to ramp to full productivity?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's the cost of slow ramp time?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would it be worth to cut ramp time by [X%]?"

7. "When deals slip or fall apart, what are the most common reasons?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How often does that happen?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's the revenue impact?"

8. "How much of your time is spent on spreadsheets, data clean-up, or reporting?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would you rather be doing with that time?"

9. "How do you measure and track team performance today?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What metrics do you wish you had better visibility into?"

10. "What's frustrating about your current [CRM / forecasting tool / process]?"
    - **Follow-up:** "What have you tried to fix it?"
    - **Follow-up:** "Why didn't that work?"

---

### For RevOps / Sales Ops Managers

**Top Pain Points:** Data silos, manual reporting, tool sprawl, integration challenges

**Questions:**

1. "You mentioned data lives in [multiple tools]. How do you keep it in sync?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How often do data discrepancies show up?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's the impact when data is wrong?"

2. "Walk me through what happens when [Sales / CRO] asks for an ad-hoc report."
   - **Follow-up:** "How long does that typically take?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How often do you get these requests?"

3. "Tell me about a recent project where you had to integrate or automate something."
   - **Follow-up:** "What was hard about it?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Did you need engineering resources?"

4. "What percentage of your time is spent on data clean-up and firefighting vs. strategic work?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Where would you rather focus your time?"

5. "How many tools are in your revenue tech stack today?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Are there redundancies or gaps?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Which tools would you replace if you could?"

6. "What's your process for adding new tools or changing your tech stack?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What makes that process hard?"

7. "Tell me about a time when data inaccuracy caused a problem."
   - **Example prompt:** Forecast miss, wrong compensation, lost deal

8. "How do you measure the ROI of your tech stack?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Which tools aren't delivering value?"

9. "What do Sales and CS teams complain about most when it comes to systems and data?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How do you address those complaints today?"

10. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your current setup, what would it be?"
    - **Follow-up:** "Why hasn't that been fixed yet?"

---

### For Executive Buyers (CRO / C-Level)

**Top Pain Points:** Revenue predictability, scaling efficiency, board reporting, strategic alignment

**Questions:**

1. "What keeps you up at night when it comes to revenue?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How confident are you that you'll hit your number this quarter?"

2. "Tell me about the last time you presented forecast to the board. How did that go?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What questions did they ask that were hard to answer?"

3. "As you scale from [current ARR] to [target ARR], what needs to change in your revenue organization?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's working today that won't scale?"

4. "How efficient is your revenue team today?"
   - **Example prompt:** Revenue per rep, CAC payback, sales cycle length
   - **Follow-up:** "Where are the biggest inefficiencies?"

5. "Walk me through your current visibility into pipeline and forecast."
   - **Follow-up:** "What surprises you (good or bad) at the end of each quarter?"

6. "What's your biggest concern about hitting your growth targets this year?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would need to happen to mitigate that risk?"

7. "How much time do your sales leaders spend on reporting vs. selling and coaching?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's the opportunity cost of that time?"

8. "Tell me about a deal that fell apart recently that you thought was 'safe.'"
   - **Follow-up:** "Why did it fall apart? What signals did you miss?"

9. "If we're talking a year from now and your revenue org is performing at the highest level, what does that look like?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would have changed? What metrics improved?"

10. "What would make this a no-brainer investment for you?"
    - **Follow-up:** "What ROI or payback period would you need to see?"

## Implication Questions

### Purpose

Amplify the consequences and costs of the problems uncovered in Problem questions. These questions help the customer realize the urgency and magnitude of the pain.

### Guidelines

- Build on specific problems already identified (don't introduce new problems here)
- Quantify impact in business terms (time, money, revenue, risk)
- Connect problems to strategic goals and metrics they care about
- Create urgency by exploring cost of inaction

---

## Implication Questions by Pain Point

### Time & Productivity Waste

**Pain:** Spending too much time on manual work (forecasting, reporting, data entry)

**Questions:**

1. "You mentioned you spend [X hours per week] on [manual process]. Let's do some quick math - over a year, that's [Y hours]. What's that time worth?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What could you accomplish with that time back?"

2. "If your entire team is spending [X hours] on this, what's the total cost to the organization?"
   - **Calculation:** [Hours] × [# people] × [loaded hourly rate] = $[Z]

3. "Beyond your time, what's the opportunity cost? What aren't you doing because you're stuck in [manual process]?"
   - **Example prompts:** Coaching, strategic work, closing deals

4. "How does spending time on [manual process] impact your ability to hit your goals?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's at stake if you miss your number?"

5. "If this doesn't get solved, what will happen as your team grows?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Does this scale, or does it get worse?"

---

### Forecast & Pipeline Inaccuracy

**Pain:** Unreliable forecast, deals falling through, surprises at quarter-end

**Questions:**

1. "You mentioned forecast accuracy is a challenge. What's the business impact when forecast is off by [X%]?"
   - **Example impacts:** Overhiring, budget overruns, missed board expectations

2. "Walk me through what happened the last time you missed forecast. What was the fallout?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How did that impact your credibility with the CEO or board?"

3. "When deals slip or fall apart unexpectedly, what's the scramble like at the end of the quarter?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What does that cost in terms of discounting, rushed decisions, or team morale?"

4. "If you can't trust your pipeline data, how do you make hiring and investment decisions?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Have you over-hired or under-hired because of bad data?"

5. "What's the cost of a deal that falls apart in the final stages?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How many deals like that happen per quarter?"
   - **Calculation:** [# of lost late-stage deals] × [average deal size] = $[Z] revenue at risk

6. "If forecast accuracy doesn't improve, what happens over the next 2-3 quarters?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How does that affect your ability to raise capital, hit growth targets, or retain your team?"

---

### Slow Ramp Time & Low Team Productivity

**Pain:** New reps take too long to ramp, inconsistent performance across team

**Questions:**

1. "You mentioned new reps take [X months] to ramp. What's the cost of that ramp time?"
   - **Calculation:** [# of reps] × [months below quota] × [quota] = $[Z] in lost capacity

2. "If you could cut ramp time in half, what would that enable?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How many more deals could you close? How much faster could you scale?"

3. "When reps underperform or churn, what's the replacement cost?"
   - **Include:** Recruiting, onboarding, training, lost opportunity cost

4. "How does inconsistent rep performance impact your ability to forecast?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Can you predict who will hit their number and who won't?"

5. "What happens if you can't improve productivity as you scale?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Do you need to hire twice as many people to hit the same targets?"

6. "How much time do your top performers spend helping coach and train newer reps?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's the opportunity cost of taking them off the field?"

---

### Data Quality & System Issues

**Pain:** Bad data, integration issues, tool sprawl

**Questions:**

1. "You mentioned data quality is an issue. What decisions have been made based on bad data?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What was the cost of those mistakes?"

2. "When data in [Tool A] doesn't match data in [Tool B], who's responsible for reconciling it?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How often does that happen? How long does it take to fix?"

3. "If your team doesn't trust the data, what do they do instead?"
   - **Example:** Build shadow spreadsheets, rely on gut feel, re-create reports manually

4. "How much time does your RevOps or Sales Ops team spend on data clean-up each week?"
   - **Calculation:** [Hours] × [loaded hourly rate] × 52 weeks = $[Z]/year

5. "What's the impact of having [X] different tools that don't talk to each other?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's the total cost of those tools? Are you getting ROI?"

6. "If your systems don't improve, what happens as you scale to [2x, 3x revenue]?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Will this approach work, or will it break?"

---

### Revenue & Strategic Risk

**Pain:** Missing revenue targets, losing deals, competitive pressure

**Questions:**

1. "You mentioned you're at risk of missing your number this quarter. What's the impact of that?"
   - **Example impacts:** Board confidence, valuation, next funding round, team morale

2. "What percentage of your pipeline is at risk right now?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's the dollar value of at-risk deals?"
   - **Follow-up:** "If half of those fall through, what's the impact?"

3. "When you lose deals to competitors, what's the most common reason?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would it take to win more of those?"

4. "If your team continues to operate the way they do today, can you hit your [annual / 3-year] growth targets?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What needs to change?"

5. "What's at stake if you don't hit your revenue plan?"
   - **Example prompts:** Next funding round, IPO timeline, job security, comp

6. "How does [current pain] impact your ability to execute on the company's strategic priorities?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What does the CEO or board expect from you this year?"

## Need-Payoff Questions

### Purpose

Help the customer articulate the value of solving the problem. These questions shift focus from pain to vision of success and co-create the business case.

### Guidelines

- Ask customer to describe the value (don't pitch - let them sell themselves)
- Focus on outcomes and benefits, not features
- Quantify value in their terms (time saved, revenue gained, risk reduced)
- Build consensus on success criteria

---

2. "Imagine you had [X hours] back each week. How would that change your ability to hit your goals?"

4. "What would it be worth to free up [X% of your time] to focus on [strategic priority]?"

5. "If we could automate [process], what would that enable you to do that you can't do today?"

## MEDDIC Qualification Questions

### Metrics (Success Criteria)

**Objective:** Define quantified success criteria and ROI requirements

**Questions:**

1. "How will you measure success if we move forward?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What metrics need to improve by how much?"

2. "What ROI or payback period would make this a clear win?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's the minimum threshold for investment approval?"

3. "Six months after implementation, what would need to be true for you to say 'this was worth it'?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How would we measure that?"

4. "What metrics are you personally measured on that this would impact?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How much improvement would move the needle?"

5. "If we deliver [outcome], what's the business value of that?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How would you quantify it?"

---

### Economic Buyer

**Objective:** Identify who has budget authority and final decision power

**Questions:**

1. "Who ultimately approves budget for this type of purchase?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's their biggest concern or priority?"

2. "Walk me through the approval process. Who needs to say yes?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Is there anyone who could say no and kill the deal?"

3. "In past software purchases, who signed the contract?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What was important to them in making the decision?"

4. "What does [CRO / CFO / CEO] care most about when evaluating investments like this?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How do they think about ROI?"

5. "Would it make sense to get [economic buyer] involved in our next conversation?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would they want to see or hear?"

---

### Decision Criteria

**Objective:** Understand requirements, evaluation scorecard, and what matters most

**Questions:**

1. "What capabilities are must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How will you evaluate vendors against those criteria?"

2. "What would make one vendor stand out over another?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How will you make the final decision?"

3. "Are there any technical, security, or compliance requirements we need to meet?"
   - **Example prompts:** SOC 2, GDPR, SSO, integrations

4. "What have you seen in other solutions that you liked or didn't like?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would you do differently this time?"

5. "If you could design the perfect solution, what would it look like?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's most important vs. least important?"

---

### Decision Process

**Objective:** Map timeline, stakeholders, steps, and approval chain

**Questions:**

1. "Walk me through what happens after our conversation today."
   - **Follow-up:** "What are the steps from here to signed contract?"

2. "Who needs to be involved in the evaluation?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's each person's role? Who has veto power?"

3. "What's the timeline for making a decision?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's driving that timeline? Is it firm or flexible?"

4. "Are there any steps in the process that typically slow things down?"
   - **Example prompts:** Legal review, security review, procurement, budget approval

5. "Have you purchased similar software before? How long did that take?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would make this process faster or slower?"

6. "Are there any upcoming events or deadlines that would influence timing?"
   - **Example prompts:** Budget cycle, quarter-end, new fiscal year, product launch

---

### Identify Pain

**Objective:** Confirm pain points are severe enough to drive action

**Questions:**

1. "Of all the challenges we've discussed, which is the most critical to solve?"
   - **Follow-up:** "On a scale of 1-10, how painful is that problem?"

2. "What's the cost of not solving this problem in the next [6 / 12] months?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What happens if you do nothing?"

3. "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
   - **Follow-up:** "Why is now the right time to solve it?"

4. "How does this problem impact your ability to achieve your top goals?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What's at stake if it doesn't get solved?"

5. "If you could only solve one problem, which would it be and why?"

---

### Champion

**Objective:** Confirm internal advocate who will sell for you

**Questions:**

1. "On a scale of 1-10, how excited are you about solving this problem?"
   - **Follow-up if <8:** "What would make it a 10?"

2. "If we're a good fit, would you be willing to help us navigate your organization?"
   - **Example:** Introduce economic buyer, sell internally, address objections

3. "What concerns might others in your organization have about a solution like this?"
   - **Follow-up:** "How would you address those concerns?"

4. "Who in your organization might resist change or prefer the status quo?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What would it take to get them on board?"

5. "If you were presenting this to [economic buyer / team], how would you make the case?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What proof points or data would you need?"

6. "What would make you an advocate for this solution?"
   - **Follow-up:** "What do you need to see from us?"

## Discovery Call Structure

### Recommended Flow (45-60 Minutes)

**Opening (5 min):**
- Build rapport, set agenda, clarify roles
- Permission to ask questions

**Situation Questions (10-15 min):**
- Understand current state, tools, team, process
- Transition: "Now that I understand your current setup, let's talk about what's working and what's not..."

**Problem Questions (15-20 min):**
- Uncover pain points (aim for 3-5 key pains)
- Probe each pain: "Tell me more about that..."
- Rank pains: "Which of these is most critical?"

**Implication Questions (10-15 min):**
- Quantify impact of top 2-3 pains
- Amplify cost of inaction
- Transition: "Let's talk about what it would look like if those problems were solved..."

**Need-Payoff Questions (5-10 min):**
- Co-create vision of success
- Define success criteria and ROI requirements
- Align on desired outcomes

**MEDDIC Qualification (Throughout):**
- Weave MEDDIC questions throughout (not a separate section)
- Aim to complete 80%+ of MEDDIC by end of call

**Close & Next Steps (5 min):**
- Summarize pain, impact, desired outcomes
- Confirm fit and interest level
- Propose next steps (demo, trial, stakeholder meeting)
- Book next meeting before hanging up

## Question Customization by Persona

### For Sales Leaders (VP/Director of Sales)

**Focus Areas:**
- Forecast accuracy and confidence
- Pipeline visibility and deal risk
- Team productivity and coaching time
- Ramp time and quota attainment

**Prioritize:**
- Problem questions on manual forecasting
- Implication questions on forecast misses
- Need-payoff questions on time saved for coaching

**Avoid:**
- Deep technical questions (defer to RevOps)
- Feature-level details (save for demo)

---

### For RevOps / Sales Ops Managers

**Focus Areas:**
- Data quality and integration
- Reporting and analytics burden
- Tool sprawl and tech stack efficiency
- Process scalability

**Prioritize:**
- Situation questions on tech stack and data flow
- Problem questions on manual work and firefighting
- Implication questions on opportunity cost

**Avoid:**
- Executive-level strategic questions (defer to CRO)
- End-user experience questions (defer to Sales Leaders)

---

### For Executive Buyers (CRO / C-Level)

**Focus Areas:**
- Revenue predictability and growth
- Scaling efficiency (revenue per rep, CAC)
- Board and investor reporting
- Strategic alignment and competitive advantage

**Prioritize:**
- Implication questions on business impact and risk
- Need-payoff questions on strategic value
- MEDDIC questions on decision process and success criteria

**Avoid:**
- Tactical process details (they'll delegate)
- Long Situation questions (research this beforehand)

## Post-Discovery Actions

### Immediate Actions (Within 2 Hours)

1. **Send Follow-Up Email**
   - Thank them for their time
   - Summarize top 3 pain points and quantified impact
   - Confirm next steps and scheduled meeting
   - Attach relevant resources (one-pager, case study)

2. **Update CRM**
   - Document all MEDDIC fields
   - Record pain points, metrics, and quotes
   - Update close date and stage
   - Note any risks or concerns

3. **Internal Debrief**
   - Share key insights with SE/manager
   - Identify demo customization needs
   - Flag any blockers or concerns

### Next Steps (Within 1 Week)

4. **Customize Demo**
   - Tailor demo flow to top 3 pain points
   - Prepare examples using their industry/metrics
   - Brief SE on key stakeholders and context

5. **Prepare ROI Framework**
   - Build initial ROI calculation using discovery data
   - Identify any data gaps to fill in demo/evaluation

6. **Stakeholder Engagement Plan**
   - Map buying committee
   - Plan path to economic buyer
   - Identify any additional stakeholders to engage

## Common Discovery Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Pitching Too Early

**Problem:** Jumping into features and demo before understanding pain

**Fix:** Resist the urge to pitch. Say: "I want to make sure I understand your situation first so I can show you what's most relevant. Let me ask a few more questions..."

---

### Mistake 2: Accepting Surface-Level Answers

**Problem:** Not probing deep enough to uncover root cause and impact

**Fix:** Follow up every answer with "Tell me more" or "Why is that important?" - Aim for 3-5 levels deep.

---

### Mistake 3: Asking Leading Questions

**Problem:** "Wouldn't it be great if you could...?" (telegraphs the answer you want)

**Fix:** Ask open-ended questions: "What would be different if...?" "How would that impact...?"

---

### Mistake 4: Talking More Than Listening

**Problem:** Dominating the conversation, not giving customer space to think

**Fix:** 80/20 rule - Customer talks 80%, you talk 20%. Use silence after questions.

---

### Mistake 5: Skipping Quantification

**Problem:** Identifying pain but not quantifying business impact

**Fix:** Always ask: "What's that costing you in time, money, or revenue?" Use Implication questions to quantify.

---

### Mistake 6: Not Confirming Champion Strength

**Problem:** Assuming the contact is a champion when they're just a tire-kicker

**Fix:** Assess enthusiasm (scale 1-10), willingness to advocate, and access to economic buyer.

---

### Mistake 7: Forgetting to Close with Next Steps

**Problem:** Great discovery call but no clear next step or meeting booked

**Fix:** Always close with: "Based on what we discussed, here's what I'd recommend next..." Book meeting before hanging up.
