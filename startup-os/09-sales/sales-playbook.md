## Sales Methodology

### Our Approach: MEDDIC + Value Selling

We use a hybrid methodology combining MEDDIC qualification with value-based selling:

**MEDDIC Framework:**
- **M**etrics - Quantified business impact and success criteria
- **E**conomic Buyer - Person with budget authority and power to sign
- **D**ecision Criteria - Requirements and evaluation process
- **D**ecision Process - Timeline, steps, stakeholders involved
- **I**dentify Pain - Business problems we solve
- **C**hampion - Internal advocate who sells for us

**Value Selling Principles:**
- Lead with customer outcomes, not product features
- Quantify business impact in customer's terms
- Build business case jointly with champion
- Differentiate on value delivered, not price
- Align solution to strategic business initiatives

## Stage 1: Prospecting

### Objective

Generate qualified meetings with target personas who fit our ICP and have an active need.

### Activities

**Outbound Prospecting:**
1. **Account Research**
   - Identify target accounts using [PLACEHOLDER - ZoomInfo, LinkedIn Sales Navigator]
   - Review company signals: funding, hiring, tech stack, news
   - Map org chart: identify champion and economic buyer
   - Research trigger events: [PLACEHOLDER - new CRO, missed earnings, rapid growth]

2. **Sequence Execution**
   - **Email Sequence:** 5-touch sequence over 10 days
     - Touch 1 (Day 0): Problem-focused email, personalized insight
     - Touch 2 (Day 2): Value prop aligned to persona pain point
     - Touch 3 (Day 5): Customer story / case study
     - Touch 4 (Day 7): Breakup email with last value offer
     - Touch 5 (Day 10): Alternative channel (LinkedIn, phone)
   
   - **LinkedIn Sequence:**
     - Connection request with personalized note
     - Comment on their posts (build familiarity)
     - Direct message after connection accepted
   
   - **Phone Outreach:**
     - Call at optimal times: [PLACEHOLDER - Tue-Thu, 10-11am or 4-5pm local time]
     - Voicemail: 30 seconds, problem-focused, reference email
     - Follow up voicemail with email recap

3. **Messaging Framework**
   - **Subject Lines:** Short (3-5 words), curiosity or value-driven
     - Example: "[Persona Pain Point] at [Company]?"
     - Example: "[Outcome] in [Time Period]"
   
   - **Email Structure:**
     - Line 1: Personalized observation (recent news, post, achievement)
     - Line 2-3: Relevant pain point question
     - Line 4-5: Brief value prop (outcome, not features)
     - Line 6: Soft CTA (quick call, share resource)
     - Signature: Keep simple, include calendar link
   
   - **Call Opening:**
     - "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. Do you have 30 seconds?"
     - [If yes] "I'm calling because we help [persona] at companies like [similar company] solve [pain point]. Is this something you're currently working on?"
     - [If no] "No problem - when's a better time to reach you?"

**Inbound Lead Follow-Up:**
1. **Speed to Lead:** Respond within 5 minutes during business hours
2. **Qualification:** Use BANT framework (see below) in first conversation
3. **Routing:** SDR qualifies, routes to appropriate AE based on segment/territory
4. **Meeting Set:** Book discovery call within 48 hours of qualification

### Qualification: BANT Framework

Use BANT for initial qualification (lightweight, fast):

**Budget:**
- "What's the budget range you're considering for this type of solution?"
- "Is there an existing line item, or would this require new budget approval?"
- Target: Confirm budget ≥ [PLACEHOLDER - $10K/year minimum]

**Authority:**
- "Who else is involved in evaluating and selecting this type of solution?"
- "What's the approval process for a purchase like this?"
- Target: Identify economic buyer (typically CRO, VP Sales) and champion

**Need:**
- "What problem are you trying to solve?"
- "What's the business impact if this problem isn't solved?"
- Target: Confirm fit with our value prop and ICP pain points

**Timeline:**
- "When do you need to have a solution in place?"
- "What's driving that timeline?"
- Target: Decision within 90 days (120 days max)

### Exit Criteria

**To advance to Discovery, must have:**
- [ ] Qualified meeting scheduled with champion (or economic buyer)
- [ ] BANT criteria confirmed (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- [ ] Account matches ICP (company size, industry, tech stack)
- [ ] Research completed: org chart, pain points, trigger events documented in CRM

**Disqualify if:**
- No budget or timeline >6 months
- No access to decision maker after 3 attempts
- Out of ICP (too small, wrong industry, no pain)

## Stage 2: Discovery

### Objective

Deeply understand the customer's pain points, quantify business impact, and complete MEDDIC qualification to determine if we can deliver ROI.

### Pre-Call Preparation

**Research (30 minutes before call):**
- Review account notes and prior conversations
- Research company: recent news, financials, growth trajectory
- Review personas: map attendees to persona profiles
- Review tech stack: integrations needed, replacement candidates
- Prepare tailored questions based on research

**Agenda Preparation:**
- Send calendar invite with clear agenda:
  1. Introductions and role clarity (5 min)
  2. Current state and pain points (20 min)
  3. Business impact and success criteria (15 min)
  4. Next steps and timeline (5 min)
- Attach relevant one-pager or case study aligned to their industry/persona

### Discovery Call Structure (45-60 minutes)

**Opening (5 minutes):**
- Build rapport: Reference research or shared connection
- Set agenda: "I'd like to understand your current challenges, quantify the impact, and see if we're a good fit. Sound good?"
- Permission: "I'll ask a lot of questions - feel free to push back if anything doesn't apply."
- Clarify roles: "Just to make sure I understand - [Name], you're [role], responsible for [responsibility]?"

**Current State & Pain Discovery (20 minutes):**

Use SPIN framework (see `discovery-questions.md` for detailed questions):

**Situation Questions:** Understand current process and context
- "Walk me through your current process for [relevant workflow]."
- "What tools do you use today? How do they fit together?"
- "How many people are involved? What are their roles?"

**Problem Questions:** Uncover difficulties and dissatisfaction
- "What's working well? What's not?"
- "Where do things break down or slow down?"
- "What manual work or workarounds do you have to do?"

**Implication Questions:** Amplify the pain and consequences
- "What's the cost of those manual workarounds? (time, errors, missed opportunities)"
- "How does this impact your ability to [achieve goal]?"
- "What happens if this doesn't get solved in the next 6 months?"

**Need-Payoff Questions:** Co-create the vision of success
- "If we could eliminate [pain], what would that enable you to do?"
- "How would that impact your [key metric]?"
- "What would success look like 6 months from now?"

**Business Impact Quantification (15 minutes):**

Anchor discovery to measurable business outcomes:

**Identify Key Metrics:**
- "What metrics are you measured on personally?" (Champion)
- "What metrics does the CRO care about most?" (Economic Buyer)
- "What would make this project a clear win for your team?"

**Quantify Current State:**
- Time spent: "How many hours per week do you/your team spend on [manual process]?"
- Error rate: "What % of [forecasts/reports/deals] have data errors?"
- Opportunity cost: "How many deals fall through the cracks due to [lack of visibility]?"
- Revenue impact: "What's the cost of [inaccurate forecast, missed deals, slow ramp time]?"

**Quantify Desired Future State:**
- "If we could reduce time spent by 50%, what's that worth in dollars?"
  - Calculate: [Hours saved] × [Loaded hourly rate] × [# of people]
- "If we could improve [metric] by X%, what's the business impact?"
  - Example: 10% forecast accuracy improvement = $[X] prevented overages/shortfalls

**Document ROI Inputs:** (Record in CRM for proposal stage)
- Current cost/time: $[X]/month or [Y] hours/week
- Target improvement: [Z]% reduction or increase
- Value delivered: $[ROI]/month or year

**MEDDIC Qualification (Throughout Discovery):**

Complete MEDDIC framework during and after call:

**Metrics:**
- "What would make this a quantifiable success?"
- "What ROI or payback period would you need to see to justify the investment?"
- Document: Success criteria, ROI thresholds, decision metrics

**Economic Buyer:**
- "Who ultimately approves budget for this type of purchase?"
- "What's their biggest concern or priority?"
- "Can we get 15 minutes with them before we present a proposal?"
- Document: Name, title, priorities, access path

**Decision Criteria:**
- "What capabilities are must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?"
- "What other solutions are you evaluating?"
- "What would make one vendor stand out over another?"
- Document: Requirements, evaluation scorecard, differentiators

**Decision Process:**
- "Walk me through what happens after our next conversation."
- "Who needs to be involved? What's the approval chain?"
- "Are there legal, security, or procurement steps we should prepare for?"
- Document: Timeline, stakeholders, process steps

**Identify Pain:**
- Confirm top 3 pain points and rank by severity (1-10 scale)
- Map our capabilities to each pain point
- Identify which pains are "critical" vs. "important" vs. "nice to solve"
- Document: Pain points, severity, our fit

**Champion:**
- "How excited are you about solving this problem on a scale of 1-10?"
- "If we're a good fit, would you help us navigate your organization?"
- "What concerns might others have that we should address early?"
- Assess: Power, influence, enthusiasm, access to economic buyer
- Document: Champion strength (scale 1-5), potential blockers

**Closing & Next Steps (5 minutes):**

- Summarize: "Here's what I heard... [recap top 3 pains, business impact, success criteria]. Did I get that right?"
- Confirm fit: "Based on what you've shared, I believe we can help you [achieve outcome]. What we'd typically do next is..."
- Propose next steps:
  - **Option A:** Demo for champion + key stakeholders (if highly qualified)
  - **Option B:** Technical deep-dive with RevOps/IT (if integration concerns)
  - **Option C:** Meet economic buyer (if champion has access)
- Set meeting: Book on the call, send calendar invite before you hang up
- Assign homework: "Can you share [tech stack diagram, current workflow, stakeholder list] before we meet?"

### Exit Criteria

**To advance to Demo, must have:**
- [ ] MEDDIC completed at 80%+ confidence:
  - [ ] **M**etrics: Success criteria and ROI requirements documented
  - [ ] **E**conomic Buyer: Identified (name, title, priorities)
  - [ ] **D**ecision Criteria: Must-haves and evaluation scorecard documented
  - [ ] **D**ecision Process: Timeline, stakeholders, approval process mapped
  - [ ] **I**dentify Pain: Top 3 pains quantified with business impact
  - [ ] **C**hampion: Confirmed (willing to advocate, has influence/access)
- [ ] Business case framework defined: Current cost vs. value we deliver
- [ ] Demo scheduled with champion + 1-2 additional stakeholders
- [ ] Mutual action plan agreed (we do X, they do Y, by [date])

**Disqualify if:**
- No quantified pain or business case
- No champion (only tire-kickers)
- No access to economic buyer after discovery
- Solution doesn't fit need (be honest - save everyone time)

## Stage 3: Demo

### Objective

Present a problem-first, tailored demonstration that connects our solution to their specific pain points and success criteria. Confirm champion support and identify next steps toward evaluation or proposal.

### Pre-Demo Preparation

**Customize Demo (60 minutes):**
- Review discovery notes: pain points, metrics, use cases
- Map demo flow to their top 3 pains (not generic feature tour)
- Prepare data/examples using their industry, metrics, terminology
- Identify which features to emphasize vs. skip
- Prepare objection responses (common: pricing, implementation, integrations)

**Coordinate Internally:**
- Brief Solutions Engineer (SE) or demo lead on key points
- Assign roles: AE leads business discussion, SE drives product demo
- Prepare leave-behind: tailored one-pager, ROI calculator, case study

**Confirm Logistics:**
- Send reminder with agenda and attendee names 24 hours before
- Request: "Please invite anyone who needs to evaluate the solution."
- Test: Screen sharing, demo environment, backup plan if tech fails

### Demo Structure (45-60 minutes)

**See `demo-script.md` for detailed script and flow.**

**High-Level Outline:**

1. **Opening & Agenda** (5 min)
   - Recap discovery: "Last time we talked, you mentioned [pain 1, pain 2, pain 3]."
   - Set expectations: "Today I'll show how we solve those specific problems. I'll pause for questions throughout."
   - Confirm: "Does that align with what you were hoping to see?"

2. **Problem-First Demo** (30-35 min)
   - For each pain point:
     - Restate the problem: "You mentioned you spend 10 hours/week on [manual process]."
     - Show the solution: Demo specific feature that solves it
     - Quantify the impact: "This saves teams like yours an average of 8 hours/week."
     - Check understanding: "Does this address what you were looking for?"
   
   - **Recommended Flow:**
     - Pain #1 (highest severity): Deep dive, show end-to-end workflow
     - Pain #2: Key differentiator vs. competition
     - Pain #3: Quick hit, time permitting
   
   - **Demo Principles:**
     - Use their data/terminology where possible
     - Focus on outcomes, not features
     - Tell a story: "Imagine it's Monday morning and you need to forecast..."
     - Avoid feature dumping: Skip irrelevant features
     - Pause frequently: "Questions so far?"

3. **Objection Handling** (5-10 min)
   - Proactively address common concerns:
     - Implementation time: "Typical time to value is [X] days."
     - Integration: "We integrate natively with [their CRM]. Let me show you."
     - Pricing: "We have 3 tiers. Based on your needs, I'd recommend [tier]. We can discuss detailed pricing in the proposal."
   
   - **Objection Framework (LAER):** See `objection-handling.md`
     - **L**isten: Let them fully explain the concern
     - **A**cknowledge: Validate their concern
     - **E**xplore: Ask questions to understand root cause
     - **R**espond: Provide tailored response with proof

4. **Next Steps & Close** (5-10 min)
   - Confirm fit: "Based on what you've seen, does this solve [pain points]?"
   - Gauge interest: "On a scale of 1-10, how excited are you?" (8+ is good)
   - Address concerns: "What questions or concerns do you have?"
   - Propose next steps:
     - **Strong interest (9-10):** "I'd love to introduce you to our [CRO/similar customer]. Would a reference call be helpful? And should we get [economic buyer] involved in the next conversation?"
     - **Moderate interest (7-8):** "Would a trial or proof-of-concept help you evaluate fit?"
     - **Low interest (<7):** "What would need to be different for this to be a strong fit?"
   
   - **Mutual Action Plan:**
     - Our commitments: "We'll send [proposal, ROI analysis, security docs] by [date]."
     - Their commitments: "You'll [review with team, intro economic buyer, complete trial] by [date]."
     - Document in CRM and email recap within 2 hours

### Exit Criteria

**To advance to Evaluation, must have:**
- [ ] Champion confirms solution addresses their pain points (score 8+/10)
- [ ] No major technical or functional blockers identified
- [ ] Next steps agreed: trial/POC, proposal, or stakeholder meeting
- [ ] Economic buyer engaged or path to engagement confirmed
- [ ] Mutual action plan documented with dates and owners

**Disqualify or pause if:**
- Champion scores <6/10 on solution fit
- Major functional gap discovered (we can't solve their core need)
- Competitor already in late-stage evaluation with POC running
- Timeline or budget falls apart

## Stage 4: Evaluation

### Objective

Support the customer's technical and business evaluation process through trial/POC, stakeholder meetings, and ROI analysis. Build consensus across decision-makers and prepare for proposal.

### Evaluation Paths

**Path A: Self-Service Trial (Starter/Professional Tier)**
- 14-day free trial with full product access
- Automated onboarding sequence
- AE checks in at Day 3, Day 7, Day 12
- Success = Usage of core features + champion meeting at end of trial

**Path B: Proof of Concept (Enterprise Tier)**
- 30-day structured POC with defined success criteria
- SE-led setup and configuration
- Weekly check-in calls with champion
- Success = Champion confirms success criteria met, requests proposal

### POC Structure (Enterprise Path)

**POC Planning (Before POC Starts):**

1. **Define Success Criteria (with champion):**
   - Identify 3-5 specific, measurable outcomes
   - Example: "Reduce forecast creation time from 10 hours to 2 hours per week"
   - Example: "Achieve 90%+ data accuracy in Salesforce sync"
   - Example: "10+ team members actively using the platform"
   - Document in POC Agreement (1-page doc)

2. **Scope & Timeline:**
   - Duration: 30 days (can extend to 45 if needed)
   - Milestones:
     - Week 1: Setup and configuration
     - Week 2-3: User testing and feedback
     - Week 4: Results review and business case finalization
   - Participants: [List names, roles]

3. **Resource Commitment:**
   - Our side: SE for setup, AE for business check-ins, support access
   - Their side: Champion project management, 5-10 test users, admin access

**POC Execution:**

**Week 1: Setup**
- Kickoff call: Review success criteria, timeline, roles
- SE configures environment and integrations
- Champion provides data access, user list, test scenarios
- Deliverable: Configured environment, users invited

**Week 2-3: Testing**
- Users test core workflows aligned to success criteria
- SE provides training sessions (live or recorded)
- AE checks in weekly: "What's working? What's not? Any blockers?"
- Champion collects feedback and usage data
- Deliverable: Usage report, feedback summary

**Week 4: Results & Business Case**
- Final check-in: Review results against success criteria
- Quantify outcomes: Time saved, accuracy improved, adoption rate
- Build business case: ROI = (Value delivered - Cost) / Cost
- Deliverable: POC results deck + ROI summary

**POC Success Metrics:**
- All success criteria met or exceeded
- 80%+ user adoption among test group
- Champion confirms readiness to move to proposal
- Economic buyer briefed on results

### Stakeholder Engagement

**Map the Buying Committee:**

Identify all stakeholders involved in decision:

| Stakeholder | Role | Influence | Concerns | Our Strategy |
|-------------|------|-----------|----------|--------------|
| [Name] | Economic Buyer (CRO) | Final decision | ROI, risk, scalability | Exec briefing: business case, references |
| [Name] | Champion (Sales Director) | Advocate | Quick wins, ease of use | Weekly check-ins, demo support |
| [Name] | Technical Evaluator (RevOps) | Gatekeeper | Integrations, data security | SE deep-dive, technical docs |

| [Name] | Legal/Procurement | Process | Contract terms, compliance | Standard MSA, security docs |

**Engagement Plan:**
- Champion: Weekly check-ins
- Economic Buyer: Midpoint and final POC briefing (15-30 min each)
- Technical Evaluator: Setup call + ongoing Slack/email support
- End Users: Training session + async support
- Legal/Procurement: Proactive outreach with standard templates

### ROI & Business Case Development

**Work with champion to build business case for economic buyer.**

**ROI Framework:**

**Current State Costs:**
- Labor cost: [X hours/week] × [Y people] × [$Z/hour] × 52 weeks = $[Total]
- Opportunity cost: [Deals lost due to lack of visibility] × [Average deal size] = $[Total]
- Tool cost: [Existing tools being replaced] = $[Total]
- **Total Current State Cost:** $[Total/year]

**Future State (With Our Solution):**
- Labor savings: [80% reduction] = $[Savings/year]
- Revenue improvement: [Forecast accuracy → better planning → 5% revenue increase] = $[Impact/year]
- Tool consolidation: [Replace 2 tools] = $[Savings/year]
- **Total Value Delivered:** $[Total/year]

**Investment:**
- Annual subscription cost: $[Pricing tier] = $[Cost/year] (see `/04-finance/pricing-strategy.md`)
- Implementation cost: $[One-time fee or $0 if self-service]
- **Total Cost:** $[Total/year Year 1], $[Total/year Year 2+]

**ROI Calculation:**
- **Net Value:** $[Value] - $[Cost] = $[Net Benefit/year]
- **ROI:** ([Net Benefit] / [Cost]) × 100 = [X]%
- **Payback Period:** [Cost] / ([Monthly Value] - [Monthly Cost]) = [Y] months

**Example ROI Summary:**
```
Investment: $25,000/year
Value Delivered: $150,000/year (10 hours/week saved × 20 people × $75/hour)
ROI: 500%
Payback: 2 months
```

**Prepare ROI Document:** 1-2 page summary with customer's data for champion to present internally

### Exit Criteria

**To advance to Proposal, must have:**
- [ ] POC/trial success criteria met (documented results)
- [ ] Economic buyer engaged and briefed on results
- [ ] Technical evaluation completed (integrations, security, compliance)
- [ ] Stakeholder consensus (no major blockers from buying committee)
- [ ] Verbal agreement to move forward: "If the proposal looks right, we're ready to move forward."
- [ ] ROI and business case agreed with champion
- [ ] Mutual close plan: Timeline, steps, approvals, target close date

**Disqualify or extend if:**
- POC failed (success criteria not met) → Investigate why, extend or exit
- Economic buyer not engaged after multiple attempts → No deal without buyer
- Major objection from key stakeholder → Address before proposal
- Timeline pushed >90 days → Move to nurture, revisit later

## Stage 5: Proposal

### Objective

Present a compelling business proposal that articulates the problem, solution, business case, pricing, and implementation plan. Secure verbal commitment and advance to contract negotiation.

### Proposal Components

**See `proposal-template.md` for detailed structure.**

**High-Level Outline:**

1. **Executive Summary** (1 page)
   - Problem statement: [Customer's top 3 pains]
   - Proposed solution: [Our offering and approach]
   - Business case: [ROI summary]
   - Investment: [Pricing tier and total cost]
   - Recommendation: [Recommended tier and timeline]

2. **Current State & Challenges** (1 page)
   - Recap discovery: Current process, pain points, quantified impact
   - Cost of inaction: "If nothing changes, you'll continue to..."

3. **Proposed Solution** (2-3 pages)
   - How our platform addresses each pain point
   - Key capabilities and differentiators
   - Integration and implementation approach
   - Success criteria and expected outcomes

4. **Business Case & ROI** (1 page)
   - ROI calculation (see Evaluation stage above)
   - Payback period and 3-year net benefit
   - Risk mitigation: References, SLAs, guarantees

5. **Pricing & Investment** (1-2 pages)
   - **Recommended tier:** [Starter / Professional / Enterprise]
   - **Pricing breakdown:** (must match `/04-finance/pricing-strategy.md` exactly)
     - Annual subscription: $[X]/year
     - Included: [Users, usage limits, features per pricing doc]
     - Optional add-ons: [Additional users, storage, support]
   - **Payment terms:** Annual prepay (15% discount) or monthly
   - **Contract length:** 1-year, 2-year (20% discount), 3-year (25% discount)
   - **Total investment:** $[Y] Year 1, $[Z] Year 2+

6. **Implementation & Timeline** (1 page)
   - Implementation phases: Setup, integration, training, go-live
   - Timeline: [Typical X weeks from contract signature to full deployment]
   - Roles & responsibilities: What we do, what you do
   - Success milestones and check-ins

7. **Terms & Conditions** (1 page)
   - SLA: [Uptime guarantee, support response times]
   - Data security and compliance: [SOC 2, GDPR, etc.]
   - Renewal terms and pricing adjustments
   - Termination clause and data portability

8. **Next Steps** (1 page)
   - Mutual close plan with dates:
     - [Date]: Proposal review and questions
     - [Date]: Verbal approval and procurement initiated
     - [Date]: Contract review and redlines
     - [Date]: Contract signature
     - [Date]: Onboarding kickoff
   - Contacts: AE, SE, CSM for post-sale

**Proposal Delivery:**
- Format: PDF + live presentation (30-45 min)
- Audience: Economic buyer + champion + key stakeholders
- Send 24-48 hours before presentation call to allow review

### Proposal Presentation

**Structure (30-45 minutes):**

1. **Recap & Framing** (5 min)
   - "Thanks for the opportunity to present. As a reminder, we've spent the last [X weeks] understanding your challenges and validating that we can deliver [outcome]."
   - "Today I'll walk through our proposed solution, the business case, and the investment required."

2. **Problem & Solution** (10 min)
   - Briefly recap top 3 pains and business impact
   - High-level overview of how we solve each
   - Reference POC results if applicable: "In your trial, we demonstrated..."

3. **Business Case** (10 min)
   - Present ROI analysis: "Based on your current costs and our expected impact..."
   - Payback period: "You'll recoup your investment in [X] months."
   - 3-year value: "Over 3 years, the net benefit is $[Y]."
   - Risk mitigation: "We've delivered similar results for [Customer A, B, C]. Happy to connect you with references."

4. **Pricing & Investment** (10 min)
   - Recommended tier and rationale: "Based on your [team size, use case], we recommend [tier]."
   - Pricing breakdown: Walk through annual cost, what's included, optional add-ons
   - Payment options: Annual (15% discount) vs. monthly
   - **Handle pricing objections:** (see `objection-handling.md`)

5. **Next Steps & Close** (5 min)
   - "Based on what we've presented, do you see this as a good fit?"
   - "What questions or concerns do you have?"
   - Propose close plan: "If this looks good, here's what we'd need to do to get you up and running by [target date]..."
   - Confirm verbal commitment: "If we can agree on terms, are you ready to move forward?"

### Exit Criteria

**To advance to Negotiation, must have:**
- [ ] Proposal presented to economic buyer and key stakeholders
- [ ] No major objections to solution, ROI, or pricing
- [ ] Verbal commitment: "This looks good, let's finalize the contract."
- [ ] Procurement or legal engaged (contract review initiated)
- [ ] Mutual close plan with target signature date
- [ ] Opportunity updated in CRM: Close date, amount, probability 75%

**Common reasons for delay:**
- Pricing objection → Address with discounting policy or value justification
- Legal review → Provide standard MSA, redline quickly
- Budget timing → Offer flexible start date or payment terms
- Additional stakeholder approval → Schedule follow-up meeting

## Stage 6: Negotiation

### Objective

Finalize contract terms, pricing, and legal language to reach a signed agreement. Navigate procurement, legal review, and security questionnaires while maintaining deal momentum.

### Negotiation Principles

**Value-First Negotiation:**
- Anchor to value delivered, not cost
- Discount only when necessary (see discounting policy below)
- Trade concessions for value (multi-year, case study, reference)
- Never discount without getting something in return

**Common Negotiation Points:**

1. **Pricing Discount**
   - **Request:** "Can you do better on price?"
   - **Response:** "Our pricing reflects the [ROI/value] we deliver. That said, I can offer [discount type] if [condition]."
   - **Approved Discounts:** (see `/04-finance/pricing-strategy.md`)
     - Annual prepay: 15% discount (standard, no approval needed)
     - Multi-year: 20% (2-year) or 25% (3-year) discount
     - Volume: 10-25% based on user count (Enterprise only)
     - Competitive displacement: Up to 30% first year (VP approval)
     - End-of-quarter: Up to 20% (CRO approval)
   - **Trade for:**
     - Case study participation and logo usage
     - Public reference or testimonial
     - Longer contract term (1-year → 2-year)
     - Prepayment (quarterly → annual)

2. **Contract Term**
   - **Request:** "Can we start with a shorter contract?"
   - **Response:** "We can do a 1-year contract at standard pricing, or save [X]% with a multi-year commitment."
   - **Policy:** Minimum 1-year term for all tiers

3. **Payment Terms**
   - **Request:** "Can we pay monthly / quarterly?"
   - **Response:** "Yes, but annual prepay gives you 15% savings. Would that work?"
   - **Policy:** 
     - Standard: Annual prepay
     - Exception: Quarterly or monthly for Enterprise (net-30 terms)

4. **Custom Terms (Enterprise)**
   - **Request:** "We need [custom SLA / data residency / on-prem deployment]."
   - **Response:** "Let me check with our team on feasibility and pricing impact."
   - **Policy:** 
     - Custom SLAs: Available for Enterprise (may require fee)
     - Data residency: Available in [regions] (may require fee)
     - On-prem: Not supported (cloud-only)

### Legal & Procurement Process

**Contract Documents:**
- **Standard MSA (Master Service Agreement):** Use our template (approved by Legal)
- **Order Form:** Pricing, term, users, tier
- **DPA (Data Processing Agreement):** GDPR/CCPA compliance
- **BAA (Business Associate Agreement):** HIPAA compliance (if applicable)

**Security & Compliance:**
- **Security Questionnaire:** Standard response template (work with Security team)
- **Compliance Certifications:** SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, [PLACEHOLDER - ISO 27001, HIPAA]
- **Vendor Risk Assessment:** Provide financial statements, insurance certs

**Redline Review:**
- **Turnaround:** Respond to redlines within 2 business days
- **Escalation:** Engage Legal for non-standard terms
- **Common Redlines:**
  - Liability cap: We cap at 12 months fees paid
  - Indemnification: Mutual indemnification
  - Termination: 30-day notice for convenience (after initial term)
  - Auto-renewal: Standard 1-year auto-renewal with 60-day opt-out

**Procurement Navigation:**
- **PO Required:** Provide W-9, insurance certificates, vendor setup forms
- **Approval Chain:** Map out signatures needed, chase down bottlenecks
- **Timeline:** Allow 1-2 weeks for procurement (4+ weeks for large enterprises)

### Closing Techniques

**Trial Close (Throughout Negotiation):**
- "If we can resolve [objection], are we ready to move forward?"
- "What's the one thing standing between us and a signed contract?"

**Assumptive Close:**
- "Let's get the onboarding team on the calendar for [date]."
- "I'll send the final contract for signature - can you commit to signing by [date]?"

**Urgency Close (Use Carefully):**
- "If we can sign by [end of quarter], I can offer [additional discount / value-add]."
- "To hit your go-live date of [X], we'd need to start implementation by [Y]. Can we finalize this week?"

**Champion Close:**
- "You've been a great partner through this process. What do you need from me to get this over the finish line?"

### Exit Criteria

**To advance to Closed Won, must have:**
- [ ] Signed contract received (MSA + Order Form + any amendments)
- [ ] PO or payment received (or payment terms confirmed)
- [ ] Opportunity marked Closed Won in CRM
- [ ] Handoff to Customer Success scheduled (kickoff within 1 week)
- [ ] Customer data entered in billing system
- [ ] AE documents lessons learned and key customer context in CRM

**Common delays:**
- Legal redlines → Escalate to our Legal, propose compromise quickly
- Procurement bottleneck → Champion escalates internally, we provide all docs proactively
- Budget freeze → Propose flexible start date or payment terms
- Executive sign-off delay → Champion escalates, AE requests executive call
