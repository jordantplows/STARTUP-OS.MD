### Pricing Philosophy

**Core Principles:**
1. **Value-Based Pricing:** Price correlates directly to customer value delivered, not cost-plus

3. **Growth Incentives:** Pricing structure encourages expansion and feature adoption

5. **Competitive Positioning:** Premium pricing justified by superior product capabilities

- All tiers: 15% discount for annual prepayment (10.2 months price for 12 months service)

```

  YES → Include in Starter
  NO → Continue

Does feature have high marginal cost?
  YES → Enterprise only
  NO → Continue

Is feature collaboration/team-focused?
  YES → Professional+
  NO → Continue

Is feature a power user capability?
  YES → Professional+
  NO → Starter
```

### Usage-Based vs. Seat-Based Packaging

**Current Model:** Hybrid approach
- Base: Seat-based pricing (user count)
- Overages: Usage-based pricing (API calls, storage)

**Rationale:**
- Seat-based provides predictable revenue
- Usage-based aligns pricing with value for high-volume customers
- Reduces barrier to entry (no overage fees until substantial usage)

**Alternative Considered:** Pure usage-based pricing
- **Pros:** Perfect value alignment, scales infinitely
- **Cons:** Revenue unpredictability, difficult financial planning for customers
- **Decision:** Rejected for core tiers, available as Enterprise option

```
  1. Conversion rate > 14%, OR
  2. Infrastructure costs < $2/user, OR
  3. Viral coefficient > 1.5 (each user brings 1.5 new users)
```

**Decision:** No freemium tier in Year 1. Revisit at 10,000 paying customers when:
- Infrastructure costs improve with scale
- Product has strong viral mechanics
- Support costs are optimized

**Alternative:** 14-day free trial (no credit card) provides try-before-buy without ongoing cost burden

### Pricing Psychology Principles Applied

1. **Charm Pricing:** $49 vs. $50 (perception of being under threshold)
2. **Price Anchoring:** Enterprise tier anchors Professional as "good deal"
3. **Decoy Effect:** Professional tier priced to make it obvious choice vs. Starter

### Glossary

- **ASP:** Average Selling Price (actual revenue per customer after discounts)
- **ACV:** Annual Contract Value (annual revenue from a customer)
- **List Price:** Published price before any discounts
- **Net Price:** Actual price paid after discounts
- **Price Realization:** Net revenue as % of list price (measures discount impact)
- **TCO:** Total Cost of Ownership (includes price, implementation, training, support)
