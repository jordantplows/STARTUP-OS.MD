### Share Class Details

**Common Stock:**
- Issued to founders and employees
- Standard voting rights (1 share = 1 vote)
- Subject to vesting schedules
- No liquidation preference
- Subordinate to Preferred Stock in liquidation

**Preferred Stock (Series Seed):**
- Issued to Seed investors
- Preferred rights: 1x non-participating liquidation preference
- Conversion: Converts to Common at 1:1 ratio
- Voting: Votes with Common on as-if-converted basis
- Anti-dilution: Broad-based weighted average
- Participating: No (clean 1x preference, then pro-rata with common)

**Stock Options (Incentive Stock Options - ISOs):**
- Granted from Employee Option Pool
- Exercise price: Fair Market Value (FMV) at grant date
- Vesting: Typically 4 years with 1-year cliff
- Post-termination exercise: 90 days (ISO rules)
- Subject to 409A valuation requirements

## Stock Option Plan Mechanics

### Plan Structure

### Vesting Mechanics

**Standard Vesting Schedule:**
- **Cliff:** 25% vests after 12 months
- **Monthly Vesting:** Remaining 75% vests monthly over 36 months
- **Total:** 4 years (48 months) to full vest

**Example: 48,000 Option Grant**
```
Month 0-11: 0 vested (cliff period)
Month 12: 12,000 vested (25% cliff vest)
Month 13: 13,000 vested (1,000 additional)
Month 14: 14,000 vested (1,000 additional)
...
Month 48: 48,000 fully vested
```

**Acceleration Provisions:**

**Single-Trigger Acceleration (Not Standard):**
- Vesting accelerates upon change of control (M&A)
- Typically NOT included in standard grants (creates misalignment)

**Double-Trigger Acceleration (Standard for Executives):**
- Vesting accelerates upon: (1) Change of control AND (2) Termination without cause or resignation for good reason within 12 months
- Example: Company acquired, new owner eliminates VP role → 50-100% accelerates
- Protects key employees in M&A scenarios

**Founder Acceleration (Reverse Vesting):**
- Founders subject to 4-year reverse vesting
- Upon termination: Company has right to repurchase unvested shares at cost
- Double-trigger acceleration: 50-100% accelerates upon change of control + termination

### Exercise Mechanics

**Exercise Price:**
- Set at FMV (409A valuation) on grant date
- ISOs: Must be ≥ FMV (409A compliant)
- NSOs: Can be < FMV but creates taxable income

**Exercise Methods:**
- **Cash Exercise:** Employee pays cash to exercise, receives shares
- **Cashless Exercise:** Broker sells shares immediately upon exercise (if liquid market)
- **Net Exercise:** Company withholds shares to cover exercise cost (post-IPO only)

**Post-Termination Exercise Window:**
- Standard: 90 days post-termination (ISO requirement)
- Extended Window (Optional): 10 years post-termination for vested options
  - Converts to NSO (loses ISO tax treatment)
  - More employee-friendly, becoming industry standard

**Tax Treatment:**

**ISOs (Incentive Stock Options):**
- No tax at grant
- No ordinary income tax at exercise (but AMT may apply)
- Long-term capital gains at sale (if hold >1 year post-exercise and >2 years post-grant)
- Favorable tax treatment but subject to $100K annual limit

**NSOs (Non-Qualified Stock Options):**
- No tax at grant
- Ordinary income tax at exercise (FMV - Exercise Price)
- Capital gains at sale (on appreciation since exercise)

**Example:**
```
Option Grant: 10,000 shares @ $1.00 exercise price
Exercise: FMV = $5.00
Sale: $10.00

ISO Tax (if qualifications met):
- Exercise: $0 ordinary income (but $40K AMT income)
- Sale: $90K long-term capital gains (taxed at 20% = $18K)
- Total tax: $18K (20% effective rate)

NSO Tax:
- Exercise: $40K ordinary income (taxed at 37% = $14.8K)
- Sale: $50K capital gains (taxed at 20% = $10K)
- Total tax: $24.8K (27.6% effective rate)
```

## Tax & Compliance

### 409A Valuations

**Purpose:**
- Determine Fair Market Value (FMV) of common stock
- Set option exercise prices (must be ≥ FMV)
- IRS compliance (avoid penalties for below-market grants)

**Frequency:**
- Required: Annually or upon material event
- Material events: Funding round, M&A offer, major revenue milestone

**Process:**
- Engage independent valuation firm ([PLACEHOLDER - 409A Valuation Firm])
- Provide financial data (P&L, cap table, projections, comparables)
- Receive valuation report (typically 2-4 weeks)
- Board approves and adopts FMV for option grants

**Valuation Methods:**
- **Early Stage:** Comparable transactions (recent funding rounds)
- **Growth Stage:** Hybrid (market approach + income approach)
- **Late Stage:** OPM (Option Pricing Model) or PWERM (Probability-Weighted Expected Return Method)

### Stock Option Reporting

**IRS Form 3921 (ISO Exercise):**
- Filed annually for ISO exercises
- Reports: Employee name, exercise date, FMV, shares exercised

**IRS Form 3922 (ESPP Transfer):**
- If offering Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP)
- Reports transfers under ESPP

**State Compliance:**
- Delaware (incorporation state): Annual franchise tax, annual report
- California (if employees in CA): Report stock option grants to FTB

### Section 83(b) Elections

**Purpose:**
- Elect to pay taxes at grant (when value is low) rather than at vest
- Avoids large tax bill when shares vest at higher value

**When to File:**
- Restricted Stock Grants (founders, early employees with unvested shares)
- **Must file within 30 days of grant** (strict deadline)

**Example:**
```
Founder receives 5M shares at $0.001/share, subject to 4-year vesting
Value at grant: $5,000

Without 83(b):
- Year 1 vest (1.25M shares): Taxed on $1.25M x $1.00 FMV = $1.25M income (37% tax = $462K)
- Year 4 fully vested: Taxed on $5M x $5.00 FMV = $25M income (huge tax bill)

With 83(b):
- File within 30 days of grant
- Pay tax on $5,000 value immediately (at $0.001/share)
- No additional tax at vesting
- Only capital gains tax at sale
```

**[ASSUMPTION]** All founders and early employees with restricted stock file 83(b) elections.

## Equity Compensation Best Practices

### Offer Letter Equity Language

**Standard Language:**
```
Subject to approval by the Board of Directors, you will be granted an option to purchase [X] shares of the Company's Common Stock at an exercise price equal to the fair market value per share on the date of grant, as determined by the Board. This option will vest over four years, with 25% vesting after one year and the remainder vesting monthly thereafter, subject to your continued employment.

Your equity grant represents approximately [Y]% of the Company's fully diluted capitalization as of [Date]. Please note that this percentage is subject to dilution from future equity issuances, including but not limited to option grants, fundraising, and other corporate actions.
```

### Equity Communication Guidelines

**At Offer Stage:**
- Share percentage of fully diluted cap table (not just share count)
- Explain vesting schedule and cliff
- Provide context: "This is typical for [role/level] at our stage"
- Share expected future dilution (Series A, B projections)
- Provide simple exit scenarios to illustrate value

**At Grant:**
- Share most recent 409A valuation and exercise price
- Explain tax implications (ISO vs. NSO)
- Provide resources: Equity compensation guides, tax advisors
- Set expectations: "Early-stage equity is high-risk, high-reward"

**Ongoing:**
- Annual equity summaries: Vested vs. unvested, current FMV, potential value
- Transparent communication at funding rounds: Explain dilution
- Refresh grants to high performers (maintain motivation)

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Under-sizing option pool:** Running out of equity for key hires → Emergency dilution
2. **Inconsistent offers:** Paying top-of-range to early hires, bottom-of-range to later hires → Resentment
3. **Ignoring 409A compliance:** Late exercises at below-FMV → IRS penalties
4. **No acceleration for executives:** Key exec leaves post-acquisition with no vest acceleration → Lose talent
5. **90-day exercise window:** Early employee can't afford to exercise → Loses equity
6. **Overpromising equity value:** "This will be worth $10M!" → Fails to materialize → Distrust

## Appendix A: Cap Table Calculation Methodology

### Fully Diluted Calculation

**Formula:**
```
Fully Diluted Shares = Outstanding Shares + Option Pool (Total Reserved)

Where:
- Outstanding Shares = Common + Preferred shares issued
- Option Pool = All reserved options (vested, unvested, and unallocated)
```

**Example (Post-Seed):**
```
Common Stock (Founders): 9,000,000
Preferred Stock (Seed): 1,000,000
Outstanding Shares: 10,000,000

Option Pool Reserved: 1,111,111
Fully Diluted: 11,111,111
```

**Note:** Some methodologies use "Fully Diluted Outstanding" which includes only allocated/vested options. We use "Fully Diluted Reserved" which includes all reserved options (more conservative, shows full dilution potential).

### Price Per Share Calculation

**Formula:**
```
Price Per Share = Post-Money Valuation / Fully Diluted Shares

Where:
- Post-Money Valuation = Pre-Money Valuation + Investment Amount
- Fully Diluted Shares = As calculated above
```

**Example (Series A):**
```
Pre-Money Valuation: $40,000,000
Investment Amount: $10,000,000
Post-Money Valuation: $50,000,000

Fully Diluted Shares (Post-Series A): 12,987,013

Price Per Share = $50,000,000 / 12,987,013 = $3.85
```

## Appendix B: Standard Terms Glossary

**Anti-Dilution Protection:** Adjusts investor's conversion price if company raises capital at lower valuation (down round). Protects investors from dilution.

**Broad-Based Weighted Average:** Most common anti-dilution formula. Adjusts conversion price based on weighted average of old and new prices, considering all outstanding shares. Less punitive to founders than "full ratchet."

**Drag-Along Rights:** Majority shareholders can force minority to sell in M&A. Prevents holdouts blocking deals.

**Liquidation Preference:** Amount investors receive before common stockholders in exit. "1x non-participating" = investors get 1x their money back, then convert and participate with common.

**Option Pool:** Shares reserved for employee stock options. Typically sized as % of fully diluted cap table.

**Post-Money Valuation:** Company valuation after investment. Post-Money = Pre-Money + Investment.

**Preferred Stock:** Stock class with special rights (liquidation preference, anti-dilution, voting). Issued to investors.

**Pro-Rata Rights:** Right to invest in future rounds to maintain ownership %. Protects investors from dilution.

**Protective Provisions:** Certain company actions require preferred stockholder approval. Protects investor interests.

**Tag-Along Rights:** If founders sell shares, investors can sell proportionally. Protects minority from being left behind.

**Vesting:** Schedule over which equity grants become owned by recipient. Typical: 4 years with 1-year cliff.
