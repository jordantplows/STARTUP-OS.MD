## Interview Process Overview

### Standard Process

| Stage | Duration | Interviewer(s) | Focus Areas | Decision Point |
|-------|----------|----------------|-------------|----------------|
| **1. Application Review** | N/A | Hiring Manager | Resume, work samples, values signals | Advance to screen? |
| **2. Initial Screen** | 30 min | Recruiter or Hiring Manager | Background, mutual fit, logistics | Advance to interviews? |
| **3. Skills Assessment** | 60 min | [Role-specific] | Technical/functional competency | Advance to panel? |

## Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric for every candidate at every stage.

### Overall Scoring Scale

| Score | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **1 - Strong No** | Significant gaps, poor fit | Do not advance |
| **2 - No** | Some strengths but too many concerns | Do not advance |
| **3 - Maybe** | On the fence, needs more data | Additional interview or do not advance |
| **4 - Yes** | Meets bar, would be good to have | Advance |
| **5 - Strong Yes** | Exceeds bar, must hire | Advance quickly |

**Hiring bar**: Candidate must average 4+ across all interviews to receive an offer, with no individual scores below 3.

## Interview Question Bank

### Section 1: Values & Culture Fit

Use these questions to assess alignment with company values from `culture-doc.md`.

#### [VALUE #1: e.g., "Bias to Action"]

**Question**: Tell me about a time you made a decision with incomplete information. What was the situation, what did you do, and what was the outcome?

**What we're listening for**:
- Did they move forward despite uncertainty?
- How did they mitigate risk?
- Did they learn and iterate, or freeze up?
- Do they own the outcome (good or bad)?

**Follow-ups**:
- What information did you wish you had?
- Looking back, would you have done anything differently?
- How did your team react to your decision?

**Scoring**:
- 5: Showed clear bias to action, learned from results, improved process
- 3: Made a decision but overly cautious or didn't learn from it
- 1: Paralyzed by uncertainty or blamed others for lack of information

---

#### [VALUE #2: e.g., "Customer Obsession"]

**Question**: Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer. What did you do, and what impact did it have?

**What we're listening for**:
- Did they proactively solve a problem, or wait to be asked?
- Do they measure success by customer outcomes or internal metrics?
- Do they show genuine empathy?

**Follow-ups**:
- How did you know this mattered to the customer?
- What did you have to sacrifice or deprioritize to make it happen?
- What feedback did you get from the customer?

**Scoring**:
- 5: Went significantly above and beyond, measurable customer impact
- 3: Did the job well but didn't exceed expectations
- 1: Focused on process over customer outcomes

---

#### [VALUE #3: e.g., "Radical Transparency"]

**Question**: Tell me about a time you received difficult feedback. How did you respond, and what did you do with it?

**What we're listening for**:
- Do they get defensive, or genuinely reflect?
- Did they act on the feedback?
- Do they show growth mindset?

**Follow-ups**:
- How did you initially feel when you got the feedback?
- What specifically did you change as a result?
- Have you sought feedback from that person since?

**Scoring**:
- 5: Embraced feedback, made meaningful changes, followed up
- 3: Accepted feedback but change was surface-level
- 1: Defensive, blamed others, or dismissed feedback

---

#### [VALUE #4: e.g., "Ownership Mindset"]

**Question**: Tell me about a time you fixed a problem that wasn't your responsibility. Why did you step in, and what happened?

**What we're listening for**:
- Do they see problems and act, or wait for someone else?
- Do they respect boundaries while showing initiative?
- Do they focus on outcomes over "whose job it is"?

**Follow-ups**:
- Did you get permission first, or ask forgiveness later?
- How did the person who "owned" that area react?
- Would you do it again?

**Scoring**:
- 5: Took initiative, collaborated well, solved the problem
- 3: Helped but needed prompting or didn't see it through
- 1: Didn't step in, or overstepped in a harmful way

### Section 2: Role-Specific Competencies

Customize these for each role. Examples below:

#### For Engineering Roles

**Technical Problem-Solving**

**Question**: Walk me through a technically challenging problem you solved recently. What made it hard, how did you approach it, and what was the result?

**What we're listening for**:
- Depth of technical understanding
- Structured problem-solving approach
- Ability to explain complex topics simply
- Trade-offs and decisions made

**Follow-ups**:
- What alternatives did you consider?
- How did you test your solution?
- What would you do differently knowing what you know now?

**Scoring**:
- 5: Deep technical expertise, clear thinking, strong communication
- 3: Competent but lacks depth or communication clarity
- 1: Struggled to explain, made poor trade-offs, or avoided hard parts

---

**Code Quality & Collaboration**

**Question**: Tell me about a time you had to give feedback on someone else's code. How did you approach it, and how did they respond?

**What we're listening for**:
- Do they give constructive, kind feedback?
- Do they focus on learning, not ego?
- Can they balance high standards with empathy?

---

#### For Product Roles

**Product Sense**

**Question**: Tell me about a product decision you made that didn't go as planned. What happened, and what did you learn?

**What we're listening for**:
- Do they use data and customer feedback?
- Do they own failures?
- Did they learn and iterate?

---

**Prioritization**

**Question**: Walk me through how you prioritize your roadmap. What framework do you use, and give me an example of a hard trade-off you made.

**What we're listening for**:
- Clear prioritization framework (e.g., RICE, ICE, value vs. effort)
- Ability to say no and explain why
- Customer-centric decision-making

---

#### For Sales/GTM Roles

**Deal Management**

**Question**: Tell me about a deal you won that you thought you'd lose. What turned it around?

**What we're listening for**:
- Persistence without being pushy
- Creative problem-solving
- Relationship-building skills

---

**Pipeline Management**

**Question**: How do you manage your pipeline? Walk me through your process and tools.

**What we're listening for**:
- Process-oriented thinking
- Use of data and CRM hygiene
- Predictability and forecasting accuracy

---

#### For Design Roles

**Design Process**

**Question**: Walk me through your design process for a recent project—from brief to final design.

**What we're listening for**:
- User research and empathy
- Iterative approach
- Collaboration with eng and product

---

**Design Critique**

**Question**: Tell me about a time you had to defend a design decision. What was the pushback, and how did you respond?

**What we're listening for**:
- Ability to articulate rationale
- Willingness to adapt based on feedback
- Balance between user needs and business goals

### Section 3: Leadership & Collaboration

Use these for senior roles or anyone who'll mentor others.

#### Leadership

**Question**: Tell me about a time you had to lead through ambiguity. What was the situation, how did you lead, and what was the outcome?

**What we're listening for**:
- Comfort with ambiguity
- Ability to set direction and rally people
- Decisiveness balanced with input-seeking

**Follow-ups**:
- How did your team react?
- What would you have done differently?
- How did you keep morale up?

---

#### Mentorship

**Question**: Tell me about someone you mentored. What did you do, and what impact did it have on their growth?

**What we're listening for**:
- Investment in others' growth
- Specific, actionable coaching
- Measurable outcomes

---

#### Conflict Resolution

**Question**: Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a teammate. What was it about, how did you handle it, and what was the outcome?

**What we're listening for**:
- Direct but respectful communication
- Focus on issues, not personalities
- Outcome-oriented (resolution, not "winning")

**Follow-ups**:
- What did you learn about yourself?
- How's your relationship with that person now?

---

### Section 4: Motivation & Fit

#### Why This Company

**Question**: Why are you interested in [COMPANY NAME] specifically? What excites you about what we're building?

**What we're listening for**:
- Genuine interest vs. generic answer
- Knowledge of our product and mission
- Alignment with values and stage

**Red flags**:
- "I just need a job"
- Can't articulate why beyond "good company"
- Interested in title/comp more than mission

---

#### Career Goals

**Question**: Where do you want to be in 3-5 years? What does success look like for you?

**What we're listening for**:
- Ambition aligned with what we can offer
- Self-awareness about strengths and growth areas
- Realistic expectations

**Red flags**:
- Wants a role we can't provide in that timeframe
- Only cares about climbing ladder, not building skills
- Expects promotion in 6 months

---

#### Compensation Expectations

**Question**: What's your target compensation range (base + equity)? [Note: Share our range first to be transparent]

**What we're listening for**:
- Expectations align with our budget
- Understands startup equity
- Focused on total comp, not just base

For final-round panel interviews, use this structure:

### Panel Interview Template (2-3 hours)

| Session | Duration | Interviewer | Focus |
|---------|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Technical/Functional Deep Dive** | 60 min | Hiring Manager + Senior IC | Role-specific skills, past work |
| **Values Interview** | 45 min | Cross-functional teammate | Culture fit, behavioral questions |

**Between sessions**: Give candidate 10-15 minute breaks

**After panel**: Debrief within 24 hours

## Debrief Process

### Immediately After Each Interview

Each interviewer submits:

1. **Overall score** (1-5 scale)
2. **Key strengths** (2-3 bullets)
3. **Key concerns** (2-3 bullets)
4. **Recommendation** (Advance / Do Not Advance / Needs More Data)

### Debrief Meeting (30-45 minutes)

**Attendees**: All interviewers + Hiring Manager

**Agenda**:

1. **Round-robin scores** (no discussion yet—just share numbers)
2. **Discuss concerns first** (start with lowest scores)
3. **Discuss strengths** (what impressed people?)
4. **Calibration**: Are we comparing to the right bar?
5. **Decision**: Advance, reject, or request more data?

**Decision rules**:

- Average score 4+: Strong hire, move to offer
- Average score 3-3.9: Maybe—discuss whether to do another interview or pass
- Average score <3: Do not advance

**Avoid**:
- Anchoring (first person stating opinion influences everyone)
- "Culture fit" as code for "I liked them" without evidence
- Lowering the bar because "we need to fill this role"

## Red Flags & Green Flags

### Red Flags (Proceed with Caution)

- **Blames others** for failures or bad outcomes
- **Can't articulate what they personally did** (uses "we" excessively without ownership)
- **Dismissive of feedback** or gets defensive
- **No questions for us** (signals lack of genuine interest)
- **Talks poorly about past employers** (even if justified, raises concerns)
- **Wildly inconsistent stories** (different versions of same event)
- **Can't explain gaps or job-hopping** without reasonable explanations
- **Overemphasizes comp/title** over mission and growth

### Green Flags (Strong Signals)

- **Owns failures** and explains what they learned
- **Asks thoughtful questions** about product, team, strategy
- **Gives specific examples** with data and outcomes
- **Shows curiosity** about our challenges
- **References teammates positively** and gives them credit
- **Has done research** on our company, product, competitors
- **Expresses excitement** about our mission and values

## Reference Check Template

Call 2-3 references (ideally including a former manager and a peer).

### Opening

"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time. [Candidate] has applied for [role] at [Company] and listed you as a reference. I have about 15 minutes—does that work?"

### Questions

1. **Context**: How did you work together? What was your relationship?

2. **Performance**: On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate [Candidate]'s performance? What made them a [X]/10 instead of higher?

3. **Strengths**: What are [Candidate]'s top 2-3 strengths?

4. **Growth areas**: If [Candidate] were on your team today, what would you coach them on?

5. **Values**: [Candidate] told us they value [X]. Did you see evidence of that? Can you give an example?

6. **Re-hire**: Would you hire [Candidate] again if you could? [Listen for hesitation]

7. **Culture**: Our culture is [describe 2-3 key attributes]. How do you think [Candidate] would thrive or struggle in that environment?

8. **Anything else**: Is there anything else I should know—positive or constructive?

### Red Flags in References

- **Hesitation** on "Would you re-hire them?"
- **Faint praise** ("They were fine")
- **Avoids answering** or redirects questions
- **Focuses only on soft skills**, avoids discussing results
- **References are junior teammates**, not managers (why?)

## Candidate Experience Best Practices

### Before the Interview

- **Send calendar invite** with video link, agenda, and interviewer names/bios
- **Share prep materials**: What to expect, what to bring, any prep work
- **Confirm 24 hours prior** via email or text

### During the Interview

- **Start on time** (respect their schedule)
- **Explain the process** (format, what we're assessing, next steps)
- **Leave time for their questions** (at least 10 minutes)
- **Be present** (no laptop multitasking, phone on silent)

### After the Interview

- **Respond quickly**: No candidate should wait >3 business days for feedback
- **Be transparent**: If you're moving forward, give timeline. If not, give honest (but kind) feedback
- **Stay in touch**: Even if not hired, they could refer others or reapply later

## Diversity & Inclusion in Hiring

### Principles

1. **Diverse pipelines**: Source candidates from non-traditional backgrounds, underrepresented groups, and varied networks
2. **Structured interviews**: Use the same questions and rubric for all candidates to reduce bias
3. **Diverse panels**: Ensure interview panels reflect diversity (gender, race, background, role)
4. **Blind resume reviews** (optional): Remove names, schools, and other identifiers in initial screening

### Avoid Bias

- **Affinity bias**: "I like them because they remind me of me"
- **Halo/horn effect**: One trait (good or bad) colors entire evaluation
- **Recency bias**: Overweighting the last candidate you saw
- **Confirmation bias**: Looking for evidence to support your initial impression

### How to Mitigate

- **Calibrate regularly**: Discuss what "great" looks like before interviewing
- **Score independently**: Submit feedback before the debrief
- **Challenge assumptions**: "What evidence do we have for that?"

## Legal & Compliance

### Questions You CANNOT Ask (US-based example—adjust for your jurisdiction)

- Age or date of birth
- Marital status, children, or family plans
- Religion or religious holidays
- National origin or citizenship (except "Are you authorized to work in [country]?")
- Disabilities (except "Can you perform the essential functions of this job with or without accommodation?")
- Arrest record (convictions may be allowed depending on jurisdiction)

**When in doubt, ask HR or legal counsel.**
