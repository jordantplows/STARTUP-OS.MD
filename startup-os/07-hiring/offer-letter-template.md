## Template Notes (Delete before sending)

### Legal Review

**IMPORTANT**: Have this template reviewed by an employment attorney licensed in your jurisdiction before using it. Employment law varies significantly by location.

Key areas to review:
- At-will employment (not applicable in all jurisdictions)
- Non-compete and non-solicitation enforceability
- Notice period requirements
- Minimum benefits requirements (sick leave, parental leave, etc.)
- Equity grant language and tax implications

### Customization Checklist

Before sending, ensure you've:

- [ ] Filled in all [PLACEHOLDERS]
- [ ] Confirmed salary and equity with hiring manager and finance
- [ ] Verified equity percentage is accurate (check with finance/legal)
- [ ] Confirmed start date works for candidate and team
- [ ] Attached or referenced any required documents (CIIAA, equity plan docs, etc.)
- [ ] Set a realistic expiration date (typically 5-7 days, up to 2 weeks for senior roles)
- [ ] Reviewed for typos and formatting issues
- [ ] Confirmed benefits listed match current company offerings
- [ ] Verified legal compliance for candidate's location

### Tone & Voice

This letter should sound:
- **Warm but professional** (excited to have them, but this is a legal document)
- **Clear and transparent** (no surprises later)
- **Aligned with your brand voice** (reference `02-brand/voice-and-tone.md`)

Avoid:
- Overly legal jargon (explain terms in plain language)
- Hype or marketing-speak (they're already convinced—be straightforward)
- Ambiguity on key terms (be specific about comp, benefits, start date)

### Equity Transparency

We recommend being transparent about:
- **Percentage ownership**, not just number of shares (meaningless without context)
- **Fully diluted cap table** as of a recent date
- **Exercise price** and how it's determined
- **Post-termination exercise period** (90 days is standard but often criticized as too short—consider extending)

Consider linking to resources like:
- [Holloway Guide to Equity Compensation](https://www.holloway.com/g/equity-compensation)
- Your company's equity FAQ or explainer doc

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Unclear vesting cliff**: Always specify that no equity vests until the one-year mark
2. **No expiration date**: Offers should have a clear deadline
3. **Overpromising on equity value**: Don't make projections about future valuations
4. **Missing contingencies**: Always include background check, references, I-9 verification
5. **Forgetting to update benefits**: Review quarterly to ensure benefits listed are current
6. **Not adjusting for location**: Benefits and legal terms vary by state/country

## FAQ for Hiring Managers

### What if the candidate wants to negotiate?

**Salary**: Have a range in mind (typically 10-15% above initial offer). Consult with finance/CEO before agreeing.

**Equity**: Harder to negotiate once Board-approved levels are set. If candidate has strong case, escalate to CEO/Board.

**Start Date**: Usually flexible. Accommodate notice periods at prior employer (typically 2-4 weeks).

**Benefits**: Typically non-negotiable (standardized across company), but consider one-time signing bonuses for unique situations.

### What if they don't respond by the deadline?

- **24 hours before expiration**: Send a friendly reminder
- **At expiration**: Offer is withdrawn (but you can extend if you want them)
- **After expiration**: Treat as a new offer (restart approval process)

### What if they decline?

- **Ask why** (compensation? role? timing? other offer?)
- **Counteroffer if appropriate** (but don't get into a bidding war)
- **Part on good terms** (they may refer others or reapply later)
- **Document feedback** to improve future offers

### What if they accept verbally but don't sign?

Verbal acceptance is not binding. Don't cancel other candidates or announce the hire until you have a signed offer letter.
