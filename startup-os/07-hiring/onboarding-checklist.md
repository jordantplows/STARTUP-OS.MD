# Onboarding Checklist

> The first 90 days set the tone for an employee's entire tenure. Use this checklist to ensure every new hire has a world-class onboarding experience.

---

**Our approach:**

[PLACEHOLDER: 2-3 sentences on your onboarding philosophy. E.g., "We believe onboarding is not just HR paperwork—it's how we set new hires up for success. ## Week 1: Orientation & Culture Immersion

**Goal**: New hire feels welcome, understands our mission and values, meets the team, and completes initial setup.

### Day 1: Welcome & Setup

**Morning**

- [ ] **9:00 AM: Welcome email from CEO** (or team-wide Slack message announcing new hire)
- [ ] **9:30 AM: HR Orientation (60 min)**
  - [ ] Company overview and mission
  - [ ] Review culture doc and values (`culture-doc.md`)
  - [ ] Benefits overview and enrollment
  - [ ] Payroll and PTO policies
  - [ ] Tools and systems walkthrough
  - [ ] IT setup: laptop, accounts, passwords
- [ ] **10:45 AM: 1:1 with Manager (60 min)**
  - [ ] Get to know you conversation
  - [ ] Review 30-60-90 day plan
  - [ ] Set expectations and success criteria
  - [ ] Answer any questions
- [ ] **12:00 PM: Team Lunch** (in-person or virtual)
  - [ ] Meet the team in a casual setting

**Afternoon**

- [ ] **1:00 PM: Product Demo & Training (60 min)** (Product Manager or Founder)
  - [ ] Walkthrough of the product
  - [ ] Key features and customer use cases
  - [ ] Competitive landscape
  - [ ] Roadmap and strategy
- [ ] **2:15 PM: Onboarding Buddy Intro (30 min)**
  - [ ] Meet your buddy
  - [ ] Get the "unofficial" onboarding (what you really need to know)
  - [ ] Set up recurring coffee chats (weekly for first month)
- [ ] **3:00 PM: Self-guided setup time**
  - [ ] Complete any remaining IT setup
  - [ ] Explore internal docs (handbook, wiki, culture doc)
  - [ ] Set up development environment (if engineering)
  - [ ] Join Slack channels, introduce yourself in #general

**End of Day**

- [ ] **Check-in with Manager (15 min)**: How was Day 1? Any blockers?

---

### Day 2: Deep Dive into Product & Customers

- [ ] **9:00 AM: Customer Success / Sales Overview (60 min)**
  - [ ] Who are our customers?
  - [ ] What problems do we solve for them?
  - [ ] Review 2-3 customer case studies or testimonials
  - [ ] Listen to recorded sales calls or customer interviews
- [ ] **10:30 AM: Company Strategy & Goals (60 min)** (CEO or Founder)
  - [ ] Vision: Where we're headed (3-5 years)
  - [ ] Mission: What we're doing now
  - [ ] Current goals and key metrics (OKRs, KPIs)
  - [ ] How this role contributes to company success
- [ ] **12:00 PM: Lunch with cross-functional teammates**
- [ ] **1:00 PM: Read key documents**
  - [ ] Company handbook
  - [ ] Brand strategy: `02-brand/brand-strategy.md`
  - [ ] Lean canvas: `01-foundation/lean-canvas.md`
  - [ ] Last quarter's board deck or all-hands presentation
- [ ] **3:00 PM: Set up in tools specific to your role**
  - Engineers: Clone repos, run the app locally
  - Product: Review roadmap, set up analytics dashboards
  - Sales: CRM training, review pitch deck and collateral
  - Design: Access design system, Figma files
- [ ] **End of Day: Reflection** (self-guided)
  - [ ] Write down 3 things you learned today
  - [ ] Write down 3 questions you still have

### Day 3: Meet the Team & Dive into Work

- [ ] **9:00 AM: 1:1 with [Key Stakeholder #1] (30 min)**
  - [PLACEHOLDER: E.g., "Head of Engineering" or "Sales Lead"]

- [ ] **10:00 AM: 1:1 with [Key Stakeholder #2] (30 min)**

### Day 4: Hands-On Work & Culture Deep Dive

- [ ] **9:00 AM: Start working on onboarding project** (self-guided)

- [ ] **End of Day: Reflection**
  - [ ] What's one thing that surprised you this week?
  - [ ] What's one thing you're excited to work on?

## Weeks 2-4: Ramp Up & Ship First Win

**Goal**: Learn the tools and workflows, build relationships, ship your first meaningful contribution.

### Week 2

**Manager Check-ins**: 2x per week (30 min each)

**Focus areas**:
- [ ] **Deepen product knowledge**
  - [ ] Use the product daily (become a power user)
  - [ ] Set up a test account and explore all features
  - [ ] Read user feedback and support tickets
- [ ] **Learn the codebase / systems** (role-specific)
  - Engineers: Read architecture docs, review recent PRs, understand key modules
  - Product: Review analytics, understand key metrics and how they're tracked
  - Sales: Learn the pitch deck, objection handling, competitive positioning
  - Design: Study the design system, understand brand guidelines
- [ ] **Attend key recurring meetings**
  - All-hands, team standups, sprint planning, design critiques, sales pipeline reviews, etc.
- [ ] **Continue onboarding project**
  - [ ] Target: Make significant progress or ship by end of Week 2
  - [ ] Ask for feedback and iterate
- [ ] **Coffee chats** (schedule 2-3 this week)
  - [ ] Get to know teammates outside your immediate team
  - [ ] Learn about their roles, how you'll collaborate

**Deliverable by end of Week 2**:
- [ ] Ship your first small contribution (feature, design, deal, content, etc.)
- [ ] Share in team Slack channel or demo at standup

---

### Week 3

**Manager Check-ins**: 2x per week (30 min each)

**Focus areas**:
- [ ] **Own a project end-to-end**
  - [ ] Take on a slightly larger task (should take 1-2 weeks)
  - [ ] Practice the full workflow: planning, execution, review, ship
- [ ] **Understand team processes**
  - [ ] How do we prioritize work?
  - [ ] How do we run sprints / set goals?
  - [ ] How do we give and receive feedback?
  - [ ] How do we measure success?
- [ ] **Deep dive into one area**
  - Engineers: Pick one system or feature and become the expert
  - Product: Run a customer interview or review user research
  - Sales: Shadow 3+ customer calls
  - Design: Contribute to a design critique or review
- [ ] **Contribute to team rituals**
  - [ ] Present at standup
  - [ ] Give feedback in retro
  - [ ] Share a learning in team Slack

**Deliverable by end of Week 3**:
- [ ] Ship a meaningful contribution (visible to customers or internal stakeholders)
- [ ] Get feedback from 2-3 teammates

---

### Week 4

**Manager Check-ins**: 1-2x per week (30 min each)

**Focus areas**:
- [ ] **Ramp up to 50% productivity**
  - [ ] You should be contributing meaningfully to team goals
  - [ ] Less hand-holding, more autonomy
- [ ] **Establish working norms**
  - [ ] Set recurring 1:1s with key collaborators
  - [ ] Clarify communication preferences (Slack, email, meetings)
  - [ ] Understand when to ask for help vs. figure it out yourself
- [ ] **Get feedback**
  - [ ] Ask your manager: How am I doing? What should I focus on?
  - [ ] Ask your buddy: What do I need to know that no one's telling me?
  - [ ] Ask teammates: How can I be a better collaborator?
- [ ] **Start thinking beyond onboarding**
  - [ ] What do you want to own in the next 30-60 days?
  - [ ] What skills do you want to develop?

**Deliverable by end of Week 4**:
- [ ] Complete onboarding project (if not done already)
- [ ] Present learnings to team or manager
- [ ] Identify 1-2 areas you want to own or improve in Weeks 5-8

---

### 30-Day Check-in (End of Week 4)

**Meeting with Manager (60 min)**

**Agenda**:
1. **Reflection**: How has your first month been? What's going well? What's been challenging?
2. **Feedback**: Manager shares formal feedback on performance so far
3. **Goals**: Align on priorities for Weeks 5-8 (the next 30 days)
4. **Development**: What skills or areas do you want to grow in?
5. **Culture fit**: How are you feeling about the team and company culture?

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] Written summary of 30-day accomplishments (new hire prepares)
- [ ] Updated 60-90 day plan (manager prepares)

## Weeks 5-8: Increase Ownership & Impact

**Goal**: Ramp up to 70-80% productivity, own projects with minimal oversight, start mentoring newer teammates (if applicable).

### Week 5-6

**Manager Check-ins**: Weekly (30 min)

**Focus areas**:
- [ ] **Own a larger project**
  - [ ] Scope: Should take 2-4 weeks and have measurable impact
  - [ ] Practice: Planning, stakeholder management, execution, iteration
- [ ] **Deepen cross-functional collaboration**
  - [ ] Work closely with another team (eng + product, sales + marketing, etc.)
  - [ ] Understand how decisions are made across teams
- [ ] **Start contributing to team strategy**
  - [ ] Share ideas in planning meetings
  - [ ] Propose improvements to processes or workflows
  - [ ] Contribute to OKRs or sprint planning
- [ ] **Develop expertise in one area**
  - [ ] Become the go-to person for a specific feature, customer segment, or skill

**Deliverable by end of Week 6**:
- [ ] Ship a project that moves a key metric or solves a customer pain point
- [ ] Document what you learned and share with team

---

### Week 7-8

**Manager Check-ins**: Weekly (30 min)

**Focus areas**:
- [ ] **Operate with increasing autonomy**
  - [ ] Identify and scope your own work
  - [ ] Make decisions with less input from manager
  - [ ] Proactively unblock yourself
- [ ] **Help onboard the next new hire** (if applicable)
  - [ ] Be an onboarding buddy
  - [ ] Share what you wish you'd known in Week 1
- [ ] **Contribute to culture**
  - [ ] Share learnings in team meetings
  - [ ] Suggest improvements to processes or docs
  - [ ] Live the company values in day-to-day work

**Deliverable by end of Week 8**:
- [ ] Complete a project that demonstrates full competency in your role
- [ ] Begin contributing to longer-term initiatives (not just tactical work)

---

### 60-Day Check-in (End of Week 8)

**Meeting with Manager (60 min)**

**Agenda**:
1. **Progress review**: What have you accomplished in the last 30 days?
2. **Feedback**: Manager shares feedback; new hire shares feedback on onboarding experience
3. **Goals for Days 60-90**: What will you own? What will success look like?
4. **Development**: What skills have you developed? What's next?
5. **Culture & fit**: Are you thriving? Any concerns or blockers?

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] Written summary of 60-day accomplishments
- [ ] Updated 90-day goals and success criteria

## Weeks 9-12: Full Productivity & Long-Term Impact

**Goal**: Operate at 100% productivity, own significant projects, mentor others, contribute to team and company goals.

### Week 9-10

**Manager Check-ins**: Bi-weekly or weekly (30 min)

**Focus areas**:
- [ ] **Own high-impact work**
  - [ ] Lead a project with company-wide or customer-facing impact
  - [ ] Collaborate across multiple teams
  - [ ] Demonstrate strong judgment and decision-making
- [ ] **Mentor or support teammates**
  - [ ] Help newer team members
  - [ ] Share knowledge through docs, demos, or pairing
- [ ] **Contribute to process improvements**
  - [ ] Identify inefficiencies and propose solutions
  - [ ] Update docs, playbooks, or workflows
- [ ] **Think strategically**
  - [ ] Start thinking 1-2 quarters ahead
  - [ ] Propose ideas for roadmap or strategy

**Deliverable by end of Week 10**:
- [ ] Ship a high-impact project
- [ ] Begin contributing to team planning and strategy discussions

---

### Week 11-12

**Manager Check-ins**: Bi-weekly (30 min)

**Focus areas**:
- [ ] **Operate independently**
  - [ ] You're now a fully productive team member
  - [ ] Require minimal oversight
  - [ ] Proactively identify and solve problems
- [ ] **Expand your impact**
  - [ ] Take on stretch projects or new responsibilities
  - [ ] Share your expertise with the team
  - [ ] Contribute to culture and team morale
- [ ] **Prepare for your 90-day review**
  - [ ] Reflect on accomplishments and growth areas
  - [ ] Gather feedback from peers and collaborators
  - [ ] Set goals for your next 6 months

**Deliverable by end of Week 12**:
- [ ] Complete 90-day onboarding successfully
- [ ] Transition to standard performance management process (regular 1:1s, quarterly reviews, etc.)

## 90-Day Review (End of Week 12)

**Meeting with Manager (90 min)**

**Agenda**:
1. **Accomplishments**: What have you shipped in 90 days?
2. **Values alignment**: How have you lived our values?
3. **Strengths**: What are you great at?
4. **Growth areas**: What do you want to improve?
5. **Feedback exchange**: Manager shares formal review; new hire shares feedback on onboarding and team
6. **Goals for next 6 months**: What will you own? How will you grow?
7. **Career development**: Where do you want to be in 1-2 years?

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] Written 90-day review (manager prepares using performance rubric)
- [ ] Self-assessment (new hire prepares)
- [ ] 6-month goals and development plan

**Outcome**:
- [ ] New hire transitions from "onboarding" to "fully ramped"
- [ ] Move to standard 1:1 cadence (weekly or bi-weekly)
- [ ] New hire is ready to take on long-term projects and contribute to team goals

## Onboarding Roles & Responsibilities

### HR / People Ops

- Send welcome email and pre-boarding materials
- Complete paperwork and benefits enrollment
- Order equipment and swag
- Set up accounts and tools
- Conduct HR orientation on Day 1
- Check in at 30, 60, 90 days

### Hiring Manager

- Create 30-60-90 day plan
- Assign onboarding buddy
- Prepare onboarding project
- Schedule Week 1 meetings
- Conduct regular check-ins (daily in Week 1, then weekly)
- Provide feedback at 30, 60, 90 days
- Ensure new hire is set up for success

### Onboarding Buddy

- Be the go-to person for questions
- Weekly coffee chats in first month
- Share "unofficial" onboarding (what you really need to know)
- Introduce new hire to the team
- Provide informal feedback to manager

### Team Members

- Welcome new hire warmly
- Make time for 1:1 intros
- Be responsive to questions
- Include new hire in meetings and social events
- Provide feedback when asked

## Onboarding Project Ideas

New hires should ship something meaningful in their first 2-3 weeks. Here are role-specific ideas:

### Engineering

- Fix a small bug or improve a feature
- Write or improve tests
- Improve documentation or README
- Refactor a small piece of code
- Add a small feature behind a feature flag

### Product

- Conduct 5 customer interviews and synthesize findings
- Audit a feature and propose improvements
- Write a product brief for a small feature
- Update product analytics dashboard
- Review user feedback and categorize themes

### Sales

- Shadow 10 customer calls and document learnings
- Update sales collateral or pitch deck
- Research 10 prospects and add to CRM
- Close your first deal (with support from manager)
- Contribute to sales playbook or objection handling guide

### Design

- Audit a feature and propose UI improvements
- Design a small component or feature
- Contribute to the design system
- Conduct usability testing and share findings
- Create onboarding screens or empty states

### Marketing

- Write a blog post or case study
- Audit website copy and propose improvements
- Launch a small campaign or experiment
- Create social media content
- Interview a customer and write a testimonial

## Common Onboarding Mistakes to Avoid

### For Managers

- **Forgetting about them after Day 1**: Check in daily in Week 1, then regularly
- **Not assigning an onboarding buddy**: New hires need a peer, not just a manager
- **Throwing them into the deep end**: Start small, build confidence, then ramp up
- **No clear success criteria**: Define what "good" looks like at 30, 60, 90 days
- **Skipping feedback**: Give feedback early and often, not just at 90 days

### For New Hires

- **Not asking questions**: If you're confused, ask—no one expects you to know everything
- **Trying to do too much too fast**: Ramp up gradually; quality over quantity
- **Not building relationships**: Invest in 1:1s and coffee chats
- **Skipping documentation**: If you learned something, write it down
- **Being afraid to ship**: Ship small things early, iterate based on feedback
