## 3. Confidentiality and Intellectual Property

### 3.1 Confidential Information

**(a) Definition:**
"**Confidential Information**" means any non-public information disclosed by Company to Advisor, including but not limited to:
- Technical information (product designs, software, algorithms, technology)
- Business information (strategies, plans, finances, customers, suppliers)
- Proprietary information (trade secrets, know-how, processes)
- Any information marked as confidential or that reasonably should be understood as confidential

**(b) Obligations:**
Advisor agrees to:
- Hold Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without Company's prior written consent
- Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of providing the Services
- Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used to protect Advisor's own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care

**(c) Exceptions:**
Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is or becomes publicly available through no breach by Advisor
- Was in Advisor's possession prior to disclosure by Company
- Is independently developed by Advisor without use of Confidential Information
- Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction
- Is approved for disclosure by Company in writing

**(d) Compelled Disclosure:**
If Advisor is required by law to disclose Confidential Information, Advisor will:
- Promptly notify Company (if legally permitted)
- Cooperate with Company's efforts to obtain a protective order
- Disclose only the minimum information required

**(e) Duration:**
Advisor's confidentiality obligations will continue for **[3 years / 5 years / indefinitely for trade secrets]** following termination of this Agreement.

### 3.2 Intellectual Property Assignment

**(a) Company Ownership:**
Advisor agrees that all inventions, discoveries, concepts, ideas, improvements, designs, developments, software, works of authorship, and other intellectual property (collectively, "**Developments**") that:
- Are created, conceived, or developed by Advisor during the term of this Agreement, AND
- Relate to Company's actual or anticipated business, products, or services, AND
- Result from or are related to the Services or use of Company's Confidential Information

shall be the sole and exclusive property of Company.

**(b) Work for Hire:**
To the extent any Developments qualify as "works made for hire" under U.S. copyright law, such Developments shall be deemed works made for hire and owned by Company.

**(c) Assignment:**
To the extent any Developments are not deemed works made for hire, Advisor hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Developments, including all intellectual property rights therein.

**(d) Disclosure:**
Advisor will promptly disclose to Company all Developments that may qualify for protection under this Section.

**(e) Assistance:**
Advisor will, at Company's expense, assist Company in:
- Obtaining patents, copyrights, trademarks, and other intellectual property protection for Developments
- Executing documents and taking actions necessary to perfect Company's rights
- Providing testimony in legal proceedings related to Developments

**(f) Moral Rights:**
Advisor irrevocably waives any "moral rights" or similar rights in Developments in favor of Company.

**(g) Advisor Inventions:**
This Section does not apply to inventions that:
- Were developed entirely on Advisor's own time
- Do not relate to Company's business, products, or services
- Do not result from the Services or use of Company's resources or Confidential Information
- Qualify for protection under applicable state law (e.g., California Labor Code § 2870)

Advisor represents that Exhibit A contains a complete list of all inventions owned by Advisor prior to the Effective Date that relate to Company's business and that are not assigned to Company under this Agreement. If no such list is attached, Advisor represents that there are no such inventions.

### 3.3 Third-Party Rights

Advisor represents and warrants that:
- Advisor has the right to enter into this Agreement and perform the Services
- The Services will not infringe or misappropriate any third-party intellectual property rights
- Advisor will not use any third-party confidential information in performing the Services

## 5. Term and Termination

### 5.1 Term

This Agreement will commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **[12 months / 24 months / 36 months]** (the "**Initial Term**"), unless earlier terminated as provided in this Section 5.

### 5.2 Renewal

This Agreement will automatically renew for successive **[12-month]** periods (each, a "**Renewal Term**") unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least **[30]** days prior to the end of the then-current term.

**Alternative:** This Agreement will not automatically renew. The parties may agree to extend the term by mutual written agreement.

### 5.3 Termination

**(a) By Either Party:**
Either party may terminate this Agreement:
- At any time, for any reason, upon **[30]** days' prior written notice to the other party
- Immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **[15]** days of written notice

**(b) By Company:**
Company may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:
- Advisor fails to provide the Services in a professional manner
- Advisor breaches the confidentiality, intellectual property, or non-compete provisions
- Advisor engages in conduct that is harmful to Company's business or reputation
- Advisor becomes unable to perform the Services due to disability or incapacity

**(c) By Advisor:**
Advisor may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:
- Company materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within **[15]** days
- Company fails to grant the Option within **[60]** days of the Effective Date

**(d) Automatic Termination:**
This Agreement will automatically terminate upon:
- Death of Advisor
- Bankruptcy or insolvency of either party
- Mutual written agreement of the parties

### 5.4 Effect of Termination

Upon termination of this Agreement:

**(a) Cessation of Services:**
Advisor will immediately cease providing the Services

**(b) Vesting:**
- Vesting of the Option will cease as of the termination date
- Vested portions of the Option may be exercised for the period specified in the Option Agreement (typically 90 days to 1 year)
- Unvested portions of the Option will be forfeited, unless acceleration applies

**(c) Return of Materials:**
Advisor will return or destroy all Confidential Information and Company property (see Section 3.4)

**(d) Final Payment:**
Company will pay any undisputed amounts owed to Advisor (if cash compensation is provided)

**(e) Expenses:**
Company will reimburse any pre-approved, documented expenses incurred prior to termination

### 5.5 Survival

The following provisions will survive termination of this Agreement:
- Section 3 (Confidentiality and Intellectual Property)
- Section 4 (Representations and Warranties)
- Section 6 (Conflicts of Interest)
- Section 7 (Indemnification)
- Section 8 (Limitation of Liability)

- Company's negligence or willful misconduct

- Imposes obligations on the indemnified party
- Does not include a full release of the indemnified party
- Includes an admission of liability by the indemnified party

## Next Steps

### For Companies

1. **Identify the Right Advisors:**
   - Look for advisors with specific expertise you need
   - Prioritize quality over quantity (3-5 advisors is typical)
   - Seek advisors who can provide value through expertise, connections, or credibility

2. **Structure the Relationship:**
   - Define clear expectations for the advisor's role
   - Determine appropriate equity compensation based on expected contribution
   - Establish a reasonable time commitment
   - Set a defined term (typically 1-2 years initially)

3. **Customize This Template:**
   - Fill in all [PLACEHOLDERS] with accurate information
   - Specify the advisor's responsibilities and expected time commitment
   - Determine appropriate equity percentage and vesting schedule
   - Add any specific provisions relevant to the advisor's role

4. **Obtain Board Approval:**
   - Present the advisor agreement to the Board of Directors
   - Obtain approval for the equity grant and agreement terms
   - Document approval in board minutes

5. **Execute the Agreement:**
   - Have an authorized officer sign on behalf of the company
   - Ensure the advisor signs and completes Exhibit A
   - Provide executed copies to both parties

6. **Grant the Option:**
   - Prepare a Stock Option Agreement consistent with your Equity Incentive Plan
   - Execute the Stock Option Agreement within 30 days
   - Update your cap table to reflect the grant
   - File any required securities law notices (e.g., Form D if applicable)

7. **Manage the Relationship:**
   - Schedule regular check-ins with the advisor
   - Provide updates on company progress
   - Request specific help when needed
   - Track vesting and update records
   - Evaluate the relationship periodically

### For Advisors

1. **Evaluate the Opportunity:**
   - Assess whether the company and role align with your expertise and interests
   - Consider the time commitment required
   - Evaluate the equity offer against market standards
   - Determine whether the equity percentage is reasonable for the expected contribution

2. **Understand the Terms:**
   - Review the agreement carefully
   - Understand the vesting schedule and what happens upon termination
   - Clarify expectations for time commitment and specific deliverables
   - Ensure you can fulfill the obligations without conflicts

3. **Check for Conflicts:**
   - Review any existing agreements that might create conflicts

   - If the equity offer is below market, negotiate for a higher percentage

   - Negotiate the exercise period after termination (longer is better)
   - Clarify any ambiguous terms

5. **Consult Professionals:**
   - Have an attorney review the agreement
   - Consult a tax advisor about the tax implications of stock options
   - Understand when taxes will be due (typically upon exercise for NSOs)

6. **Execute the Agreement:**
   - Sign the agreement
   - Complete Exhibit A (list any prior inventions)
   - Ensure you receive an executed copy

7. **Provide Value:**
   - Be responsive and available within the agreed time commitment

## Advisor Equity Guidelines

### Standard Equity Ranges (as of 2024)

**Based on the Founder Institute's FAST Agreement guidelines and market standards:**

| **Time Commitment** | **Engagement Period** | **Typical Equity Range** |
|---------------------|----------------------|-------------------------|
| 2-3 hours/month | 1 year | 0.10% - 0.20% |
| 2-3 hours/month | 2 years | 0.20% - 0.40% |
| 4-6 hours/month | 1 year | 0.20% - 0.40% |
| 4-6 hours/month | 2 years | 0.40% - 0.80% |
| 8-10 hours/month | 2 years | 0.80% - 1.50% |
| 10+ hours/month (very active) | 2 years | 1.00% - 2.00% |

**Adjustments:**
- **Stage:** Earlier stage = higher equity (more risk)
- **Expertise:** Highly specialized or rare expertise = higher equity
- **Value:** Access to critical networks or customers = higher equity
- **Involvement:** Strategic involvement vs. tactical = adjust accordingly
- **Name recognition:** Well-known advisors may command higher equity

### FAST Agreement Alternative

Consider using the Founder Institute's FAST (Founder/Advisor Standard Template) Agreement, which uses a simple formula:
- **Equity = (Stage × Time × Level) ÷ 100**
- Stage: Company stage (early = higher multiplier)
- Time: Expected time commitment
- Level: Level of involvement and value provided

More info: https://fi.co/fast

## Red Flags to Avoid

**For Companies:**
- ❌ Granting equity to advisors who don't provide meaningful value
- ❌ Over-allocating equity (keep advisor pool under 2-3% of total equity)
- ❌ Failing to impose vesting (always use vesting schedules)
- ❌ Not clearly defining expectations
- ❌ Granting equity without board approval
- ❌ Forgetting to update the cap table
- ❌ Using advisors as pseudo-employees (creates misclassification risk)

**For Advisors:**
- ❌ Accepting equity without vesting (unfair to you if terminated early)
- ❌ Agreeing to overly broad non-compete provisions
- ❌ Failing to disclose conflicts of interest
- ❌ Accepting equity below market value for your contribution
- ❌ Not understanding the tax implications of stock options
- ❌ Signing without legal review for significant equity positions

## Best Practices

**For Companies:**
1. **Be selective:** Quality over quantity. A few great advisors are better than many mediocre ones.
2. **Set clear expectations:** Define what you need and communicate it clearly.
3. **Make it official:** Use a written agreement with vesting.
4. **Stay engaged:** Regular check-ins keep advisors informed and engaged.
5. **Be responsive:** When an advisor makes an introduction, follow up promptly.
6. **Show appreciation:** Update advisors on progress and acknowledge their contributions.

**For Advisors:**
1. **Be selective:** Only advise companies you believe in and can meaningfully help.
2. **Set boundaries:** Be clear about your time limitations.
3. **Be proactive:** Don't wait to be asked; offer insights and introductions.
4. **Stay informed:** Keep up with the company's progress.
5. **Be honest:** Give candid feedback, even when it's difficult.
6. **Add value:** Focus on areas where you have genuine expertise.

> - **Board observers:** For advisors who want more formal involvement without director responsibilities
