## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

### 1.1 Confidential Information Defined

"**Confidential Information**" means any and all information, whether written, oral, electronic, visual, or in any other form, that is disclosed by one Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party (the "**Receiving Party**"), including but not limited to:

**(a) Technical Information:**
- Product designs, specifications, and prototypes
- Software code, algorithms, and architecture
- Technical drawings, diagrams, and schematics
- Formulas, processes, and methods
- Research and development information
- Inventions, discoveries, and improvements
- Know-how and trade secrets
- Test data and results
- Performance metrics and benchmarks

**(b) Business Information:**
- Business plans and strategies
- Financial information, including revenues, costs, profits, and projections
- Pricing information and cost structures
- Customer lists and information
- Supplier and vendor information
- Marketing plans and strategies
- Sales data and forecasts
- Market research and analysis
- Business models and methods

**(c) Product and Service Information:**
- Product roadmaps and development plans
- Feature specifications and requirements
- User interface designs and mockups
- Service offerings and capabilities
- Integration plans and APIs

**(d) Operational Information:**
- Internal policies and procedures
- Operational methods and processes
- Organizational structure
- Employee information (to the extent permitted by law)
- Vendor and contractor relationships

**(e) Strategic Information:**
- Merger and acquisition plans
- Partnership opportunities and negotiations
- Expansion plans and strategies
- Competitive analyses

**(f) Proprietary Information:**
- Patents, copyrights, and trademarks
- Proprietary methodologies and frameworks
- Domain names and branding materials

### 1.2 Designation of Confidential Information

Confidential Information may be designated as confidential by:
- Marking materials as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with a similar designation
- Identifying information as confidential orally, followed by written confirmation within ten (10) business days
- The nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure making it reasonably apparent that the information is confidential

### 1.3 Forms of Confidential Information

Confidential Information may be disclosed in any form, including:
- Written documents (physical or electronic)
- Oral communications
- Visual presentations
- Electronic data files
- Prototypes, samples, or models
- Access to facilities, systems, or materials

---

## 2. Exclusions from Confidential Information

Confidential Information does **not** include information that:

**(a) Public Domain:**
- Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party

**(b) Prior Knowledge:**
- Was in the Receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by the Receiving Party's written records

**(c) Independent Development:**
- Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as evidenced by the Receiving Party's written records

**(d) Rightful Receipt from Third Party:**
- Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction on disclosure and without breach of a confidentiality obligation

**(e) Approval for Disclosure:**
- Is approved for disclosure by prior written authorization of the Disclosing Party

The Receiving Party bears the burden of proving that information falls within one of these exclusions.

## 3. Obligations of Receiving Party

### 3.1 Non-Disclosure and Non-Use

The Receiving Party agrees to:

**(a) Confidentiality:**
- Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Not disclose, publish, or disseminate Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party
- Use the same degree of care to protect the Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care

**(b) Limited Use:**
- Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose stated in this Agreement
- Not use Confidential Information for any other purpose, including for its own benefit or the benefit of any third party
- Not reverse engineer, disassemble, or decompile any prototypes, software, or other tangible objects that embody Confidential Information

### 3.2 Limited Disclosure

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to:
- Its employees, officers, directors, and consultants (collectively, "**Representatives**") who have a legitimate need to know the Confidential Information for the Purpose
- Such Representatives only after informing them of the confidential nature of the information and requiring them to comply with the terms of this Agreement

The Receiving Party shall:
- Be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives
- Take all reasonable measures to ensure its Representatives comply with this Agreement
- Limit access to Confidential Information to only those Representatives with a need to know

### 3.3 Protection of Confidential Information

The Receiving Party shall:
- Take all reasonable precautions to protect the Confidential Information
- Implement and maintain appropriate security measures, including physical, technical, and administrative safeguards
- Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information
- Cooperate with the Disclosing Party to prevent further unauthorized use or disclosure
- Take reasonable steps to recover Confidential Information and prevent further breach

### 3.4 No Copying or Modification

The Receiving Party shall not:
- Copy, reproduce, or duplicate Confidential Information except as necessary for the Purpose
- Modify, translate, or create derivative works based on Confidential Information without prior written consent
- Remove or alter any proprietary notices or markings on Confidential Information

## 4. Compelled Disclosure

### 4.1 Legal Requirements

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, court order, subpoena, or other legal process to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall:

**(a) Prompt Notice:**
- Promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing of such requirement before disclosure (to the extent legally permitted)
- Provide sufficient time for the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy

**(b) Reasonable Assistance:**
- Reasonably cooperate with the Disclosing Party's efforts to obtain a protective order or other relief
- Provide reasonable assistance at the Disclosing Party's expense

**(c) Limited Disclosure:**
- Disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed
- Use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for any Confidential Information disclosed

**(d) Notice of Disclosure:**
- Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of the information disclosed and the circumstances of the disclosure

### 4.2 No Waiver

Disclosure pursuant to this Section 4 shall not constitute a waiver of the confidential nature of the information or a breach of this Agreement.

---

## 5. Return or Destruction of Confidential Information

### 5.1 Return Upon Request

Upon the written request of the Disclosing Party or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall:

**(a) Promptly Return or Destroy:**
- Return all Confidential Information (including all copies, notes, summaries, analyses, and derivatives) to the Disclosing Party, or
- Destroy all Confidential Information and certify such destruction in writing to the Disclosing Party

**(b) Certification:**
- Provide written certification signed by an authorized officer that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed

**(c) Electronic Data:**
- Permanently delete all electronic copies of Confidential Information from all systems, devices, and storage media
- Overwrite or otherwise render irretrievable any Confidential Information stored electronically

### 5.2 Permitted Retention

Notwithstanding Section 5.1, the Receiving Party may retain:

**(a) Legal Requirements:**
- Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law or regulation
- Such information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement

**(b) Backup Systems:**
- Confidential Information in archived or backup systems, provided that:
  - Such retention is pursuant to routine, automatic backup procedures
  - The Confidential Information is not readily accessible
  - The Confidential Information remains subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement
  - The Confidential Information is deleted in accordance with the Receiving Party's standard document retention policies

### 5.3 Survival of Obligations

The return or destruction of Confidential Information shall not relieve the Receiving Party of its obligations under this Agreement.

## 6. No License or Rights Granted

### 6.1 No Intellectual Property Rights

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to grant the Receiving Party any:
- License, right, title, or interest in or to the Confidential Information
- Intellectual property rights, including patents, copyrights, trademarks, or trade secrets
- Right to use the Confidential Information except as expressly permitted for the Purpose

### 6.2 Ownership

All Confidential Information remains the sole property of the Disclosing Party. The Receiving Party acknowledges that disclosure of Confidential Information does not constitute a sale, assignment, or transfer of any rights.

### 6.3 No Obligation to Disclose

Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to:
- Disclose any particular Confidential Information
- Enter into any further agreement or business relationship
- Proceed with the Purpose

### 6.4 Independently Developed Information

Each Party retains the right to independently develop, acquire, or license information, products, or services similar to the other Party's Confidential Information, provided such development, acquisition, or licensing does not violate this Agreement.

- Merchantability
- Fitness for a particular purpose
- Non-infringement
- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Reliability

### 7.2 No Representation

The Disclosing Party makes no representation or warranty that:
- The Confidential Information is accurate, complete, or current
- The Confidential Information does not infringe third-party rights
- Use of the Confidential Information will achieve any particular result
- The Confidential Information is free from errors or defects

### 7.3 No Liability for Use

The Disclosing Party shall not be liable for any damages arising from the Receiving Party's use of or reliance on the Confidential Information.

## 8. Term and Termination

### 8.1 Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **[2 years / 3 years / 5 years]** (the "**Term**"), unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

### 8.2 Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement:
- Upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party
- Immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days of written notice
- Immediately by mutual written agreement

### 8.3 Survival of Obligations

Notwithstanding termination or expiration of this Agreement:

**(a) Confidentiality Obligations Continue:**
The obligations under Sections 3 (Obligations of Receiving Party) and 5 (Return or Destruction) shall survive for a period of **[3 years / 5 years / indefinitely for trade secrets]** following termination or expiration.

**(b) Other Surviving Provisions:**
The following provisions shall survive termination or expiration:
- Section 2 (Exclusions from Confidential Information)
- Section 6 (No License or Rights Granted)
- Section 7 (No Warranties)
- Section 9 (Remedies)
- Section 10 (General Provisions)

## 9. Remedies

### 9.1 Irreparable Harm

The Receiving Party acknowledges that:
- Disclosure or misuse of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party
- Monetary damages may be inadequate to compensate for such harm
- The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond

### 9.2 Injunctive Relief

In addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to:
- Temporary restraining orders
- Preliminary and permanent injunctions
- Specific performance
- Other equitable relief to prevent or remedy any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement

### 9.3 Cumulative Remedies

All remedies under this Agreement are cumulative and not exclusive. The exercise of one remedy shall not preclude the exercise of any other remedy.

### 9.4 Costs and Attorneys' Fees

The prevailing Party in any action to enforce this Agreement shall be entitled to recover:
- Reasonable attorneys' fees
- Court costs and expenses
- Other costs of enforcement

### 9.5 No Waiver

Failure by either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such provision or any other provision. No waiver shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the Party against whom the waiver is asserted.

## 10. General Provisions

### 10.1 Governing Law

### 10.2 Jurisdiction and Venue

Each Party irrevocably:
- Submits to the personal jurisdiction of such courts
- Waives any objection to venue in such courts
- Waives any claim that such courts are an inconvenient forum

### 10.3 Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether written or oral.

### 10.4 Amendments

No amendment, modification, or waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

### 10.5 Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable, or if such modification is not possible, the invalid provision shall be severed from this Agreement.

### 10.6 Assignment

Neither Party may assign or transfer this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that either Party may assign this Agreement:
- To a successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets
- To an affiliate or subsidiary, provided the assigning Party remains liable for performance

Any attempted assignment in violation of this Section shall be void.

### 10.7 Binding Effect

This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns.

### 10.8 No Partnership

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a partnership, joint venture, agency, employment, or franchise relationship between the Parties. Neither Party has the authority to bind the other Party or incur obligations on behalf of the other Party.

### 10.9 Independent Contractors

The Parties are independent contractors. This Agreement does not create any employment, agency, or representative relationship.

### 10.10 Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures and delivery by email (PDF) shall have the same force and effect as original signatures and delivery.

### 10.11 Notices

All notices, requests, demands, and other communications under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed given:
- Upon personal delivery
- One (1) business day after deposit with a reputable overnight courier service
- Three (3) business days after deposit in the U.S. mail, certified or registered, return receipt requested, postage prepaid
- Upon receipt if sent by email (with confirmation of receipt)

Notices shall be sent to the addresses set forth in the preamble to this Agreement or to such other address as a Party may designate by written notice.

### 10.12 Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance under this Agreement due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, war, terrorism, riots, embargoes, acts of civil or military authorities, fire, floods, accidents, strikes, or fuel crises.

### 10.13 Construction

This Agreement shall be construed fairly and reasonably and not strictly for or against either Party. Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

### 10.14 Export Compliance

The Parties shall comply with all applicable export control laws and regulations, including the U.S. Export Administration Regulations and the International Traffic in Arms Regulations. Confidential Information shall not be exported or re-exported in violation of applicable law.

### 10.15 Third-Party Beneficiaries

This Agreement is for the sole benefit of the Parties and their permitted successors and assigns. Nothing in this Agreement, express or implied, is intended to confer upon any other person any rights or remedies.

## Next Steps

### For Both Parties

1. **Review Carefully:** Read the entire NDA and ensure you understand all obligations
2. **Customize Terms:** Modify the template to fit your specific situation
3. **Consult Legal Counsel:** Have an attorney review before signing
4. **Complete Information:** Fill in all [PLACEHOLDERS] with accurate information
5. **Execute Properly:** Ensure authorized signatories execute the agreement
6. **Distribute Copies:** Provide executed copies to both parties
7. **Store Securely:** Maintain the NDA in a secure location for the duration of the term and survival period

### Key Decisions to Make

**1. Term Length:**
- Short-term discussions: 1-2 years
- Ongoing relationships: 3-5 years
- Trade secrets: Consider indefinite protection for trade secrets

**2. Survival Period:**
- Standard: 3-5 years after termination
- Trade secrets: Indefinite (or "for so long as the information remains a trade secret")

**3. Mutual vs. One-Way:**
- This template is mutual (both parties exchange confidential information)
- For one-way NDAs, remove reciprocal obligations for one party

**4. Specific Purpose:**
- Be as specific as possible about the purpose of disclosure
- Include in the main agreement or in Appendix A

**5. Exclusions:**
- Consider whether any additional exclusions are needed
- Standard exclusions are included in Section 2

### Red Flags to Avoid

- ❌ Signing an NDA without reading it thoroughly
- ❌ Using an overly broad definition of confidential information
- ❌ Failing to mark confidential information properly
- ❌ Sharing information before the NDA is fully executed
- ❌ Disclosing information to individuals not covered by the NDA
- ❌ Not training employees on NDA obligations
- ❌ Failing to track what information was disclosed
- ❌ Not implementing proper security measures
- ❌ Ignoring requests to return or destroy confidential information
- ❌ Letting the NDA expire without renewal if discussions continue

### Best Practices

**Before Disclosure:**
- Execute the NDA before disclosing any confidential information
- Clearly identify what information is confidential
- Mark confidential materials with appropriate legends
- Maintain a disclosure log

**During the Relationship:**
- Only disclose information necessary for the stated purpose
- Limit access to authorized representatives
- Use secure transmission methods
- Follow up oral disclosures with written confirmation
- Document all disclosures

**After Termination:**
- Ensure all confidential information is returned or destroyed
- Obtain written certification of destruction if applicable
- Maintain records of the NDA and compliance
- Continue to protect confidential information during survival period

### Industry-Specific Considerations

**Technology/Software:**
- Address source code, algorithms, and technical specifications
- Consider adding restrictions on reverse engineering
- Address beta testing and early access

**Healthcare:**
- Ensure HIPAA compliance for protected health information
- Address BAA (Business Associate Agreement) requirements if applicable

**Finance:**
- Address GLBA compliance for financial information
- Consider NDA requirements from regulatory bodies

**Manufacturing:**
- Address trade secrets, formulas, and processes
- Consider site visit protocols and photography restrictions

## Alternative NDA Structures

### One-Way (Unilateral) NDA

If only one party will disclose confidential information, use a one-way NDA:
- Define one party as "Disclosing Party" and the other as "Receiving Party"
- Remove reciprocal obligations
- Simpler structure, appropriate for vendor relationships

### Short-Form NDA

For less formal situations, consider a shorter version:
- Simplified definitions
- Essential obligations only
- 1-3 pages
- Appropriate for initial conversations

### Employee/Contractor NDA

For employees and contractors:
- Often combined with employment or services agreement
- Broader scope covering all company information
- May include non-compete and non-solicit provisions
- Often indefinite duration for trade secrets

> [!NOTE]
> **Important Reminders:**
> - This is a mutual NDA template where both parties exchange confidential information
> - For one-way disclosures, modify the structure to designate only one Disclosing Party
> - State laws vary regarding enforceability of certain provisions (e.g., duration, scope)
> - Some states may not enforce indefinite confidentiality obligations
> - Trade secrets may be protected indefinitely under state and federal trade secret laws
> - Always consult with an attorney licensed in your jurisdiction before using this template
> - Consider having the NDA reviewed by counsel in both parties' jurisdictions if in different states/countries

> [!TIP]
> **When to Use an NDA:**
> - Before sharing product roadmaps or technical specifications with potential partners
> - Before discussing business strategies or financial information with investors
> - Before providing customer data or proprietary processes to vendors
> - Before conducting due diligence for mergers, acquisitions, or investments
> - Before sharing sensitive information with consultants or advisors
>
> **When NOT to Use an NDA:**
> - For general business discussions that don't involve sensitive information
> - After information is already public
> - When the other party refuses to sign (consider whether disclosure is necessary)
> - For information that is industry standard or commonly known
