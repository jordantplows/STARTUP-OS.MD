# Growth Experiments

> A framework for systematically testing, measuring, and scaling growth tactics.

---

## Experimentation Framework

### Why Experiment?

[PLACEHOLDER: E.g., "Most marketing tactics fail. Instead of guessing, we run small, fast experiments to find what works, then double down on winners and kill losers quickly."]

---

### Experiment Structure

Every experiment follows this format:

1. **Hypothesis**: What do we believe will happen and why?
2. **Metric**: What are we measuring? (Primary and secondary metrics)
3. **Test Design**: How will we run the test? (A/B test, time-based, cohort-based)
4. **Timeline**: How long will we run it? (Aim for statistical significance)
5. **Resources**: What does it cost? (Time, budget, tools)
6. **Success Criteria**: What result means we scale this? What means we kill it?
7. **Results**: What actually happened?
8. **Next Action**: Scale, iterate, or kill?

---

### Experiment Prioritization (ICE Score)

We prioritize experiments using the **ICE framework**:

- **Impact** (1-10): How big is the potential impact on our north star metric?
- **Confidence** (1-10): How confident are we this will work?
- **Ease** (1-10): How easy is it to run this experiment?

**ICE Score = (Impact + Confidence + Ease) / 3**

**Priority**:

- **8-10**: Run immediately (P0)
- **6-7.9**: Run this quarter (P1)
- **<6**: Backlog (P2)

### Do's

- ✅ [PLACEHOLDER: E.g., "Start with one variable at a time (don't change headline + CTA + image in one test)"]
- ✅ [PLACEHOLDER: E.g., "Run tests to statistical significance (95% confidence, adequate sample size)"]
- ✅ [PLACEHOLDER: E.g., "Document everything (hypothesis, results, learnings)"]
- ✅ [PLACEHOLDER: E.g., "Fail fast (kill losers quickly, don't let them drag on)"]
- ✅ [PLACEHOLDER: E.g., "Compound wins (winners become new baseline, test against them)"]

- ❌ [PLACEHOLDER: E.g., "Don't stop tests early (even if it looks like a winner)"]
