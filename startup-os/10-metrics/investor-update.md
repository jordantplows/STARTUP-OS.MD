# Monthly Investor Update Template

> A proven template for monthly investor email updates that build confidence, maintain relationships, and set you up for future fundraising success.

---

## Overview

This document provides a complete template for monthly investor updates. Consistent, transparent communication with investors:
- Builds trust and confidence
- Keeps your company top-of-mind for future funding rounds
- Surfaces valuable intros, advice, and support
- Demonstrates operational excellence

**Related documents:**
- `kpi-framework.md` - Definitions for all metrics referenced
- `dashboard-mockup.md` - Visual dashboard views
- `north-star-metric.md` - Your primary success metric
- `weekly-review.md` - Internal weekly review cadence
- `../04-finance/unit-economics.md` - Unit economics details
- `../04-finance/fundraising-deck.md` - Full investment narrative

**Frequency:** Monthly, sent first week of each month

**Distribution:**
- All investors (angels, VCs, advisors who invested)
- Key advisors (even if they didn't invest)
- Optional: Prospective future investors (build relationship early)

**Format:** Email (not attachment) - easier to read, higher engagement

---

## Email Template Structure

### Subject Line Formula

**Option 1 (Metric-Driven):**
```
[Company Name] Update: [Key Metric] hits [Milestone] 🎉
```

**Examples:**
- "Acme Update: Hit $100K MRR 🎉"
- "Acme Update: 1,000th customer this month 🚀"
- "Acme June Update: Growing 25% MoM"

**Option 2 (Milestone-Driven):**
```
[Company Name] [Month] Update: [Major Milestone or Achievement]
```

**Examples:**
- "Acme June Update: Launched Enterprise Tier"
- "Acme Q2 Update: Closed Series A"
- "Acme July Update: Profitability Achieved"

**Option 3 (Simple, Consistent):**
```
[Company Name] - [Month] [Year] Investor Update
```

**Example:**
- "Acme - June 2026 Investor Update"

**Best Practice:** Use Option 1 or 2 when you have exciting news. Use Option 3 as default for consistency.

### Opening Section

```
Hi [First Name],

[ONE SENTENCE TL;DR - THE HEADLINE]

Example:
"We crossed $100K MRR this month, growing 30% MoM with improving unit economics."

or

"Tough month - we missed our revenue target by 15% due to longer sales cycles, 
but we're adjusting our approach and I'm confident we'll get back on track."
```

**Best Practice:**
- Lead with the most important news (good or bad)
- Be honest - investors appreciate transparency
- One sentence only - details come later

### Section 3: Top 3 Lowlights / Challenges

**Purpose:** What didn't go well - be transparent

**Format:**

```
⚠️ CHALLENGES

1. [BIGGEST CHALLENGE - BE HONEST]
   - [Why this happened]
   - [What we're doing about it]

2. [SECOND CHALLENGE]
   - [Context]
   - [Action plan]

3. [THIRD CHALLENGE]
   - [Context]
   - [Action plan]
```

**Examples:**

```
⚠️ CHALLENGES

1. Churn increased to 6% this month (target: <5%)
   - 8 customers churned, primarily Starter tier
   - Root cause: Poor onboarding - users not reaching activation
   - **Action plan:** Revamped onboarding flow (launches next week), 
     added CSM check-in for all new customers at Day 3

2. Sales cycles lengthening from 30 days to 45 days
   - Enterprise deals taking longer than expected (procurement processes)
   - Impacting near-term revenue (will miss this Q target by ~10%)
   - **Action plan:** Built enterprise sales playbook, hiring sales engineer 
     to accelerate technical evaluation

3. Lost competitive deal to [Competitor] on price
   - They undercut us by 40% (likely burning cash to win deals)
   - Customer cited "similar features, lower price"
   - **Action plan:** Doubling down on differentiation (AI features), 
     building ROI calculator to justify premium pricing
```

**Best Practices:**
- Always include action plans - show you're on top of issues
- Don't sugarcoat - investors value honesty
- Frame challenges as learning opportunities
- Avoid excuses - take ownership

### Section 4: Deep Dive - One Topic Per Month

**Purpose:** Rotate through different topics each month to give investors full picture

**Format:**

```
📈 DEEP DIVE: [Topic]

[2-3 paragraphs with supporting data and insights]
```

**Rotation Schedule (Example):**

| Month | Deep Dive Topic |
|-------|-----------------|
| Jan | Unit Economics (LTV, CAC, payback) |
| Feb | Product Roadmap & Feature Adoption |
| Mar | Sales Pipeline & Go-to-Market Strategy |
| Apr | Customer Success & Retention |
| May | Team & Hiring |
| Jun | Competitive Landscape |
| Jul | Fundraising Plans (if applicable) |
| Aug | Technology & Infrastructure |
| Sep | Marketing & Growth Channels |
| Oct | Financials & Burn |
| Nov | Strategic Partnerships |
| Dec | Year in Review & Next Year Goals |

**Example Deep Dive:**

```
📈 DEEP DIVE: Unit Economics

Our unit economics continue to improve as we scale:

**LTV/CAC Ratio: 5.2:1 (up from 4.1:1 last month)**
- LTV increased from $3,800 to $4,200 (lower churn, higher ARPA)
- CAC decreased from $950 to $800 (marketing efficiency gains)
- Now well above the 3:1 threshold for healthy SaaS

**CAC Payback: 6.2 months (down from 8.1 months)**
- Improved activation rate (75% → 82%) driving faster payback
- On track to hit <6 month payback by Q3

**NRR: 98% (moving toward 100%+ target)**
- Expansion MRR up 40% this month (upsells working)
- Churn improved from 6% to 4.5% (onboarding fixes paying off)
- Expect to hit >100% NRR by Q3, which would put us in top quartile

**What this means:** We're ready to scale S&M investment aggressively. 
Planning to double marketing spend next quarter to capitalize on strong 
unit economics.

See attached unit economics dashboard for full details.
```

## Best Practices for Investor Updates

### Do's

1. **Be Consistent**
   - Send on same day each month (e.g., first Monday)
   - Use same format so investors know what to expect
   - Don't skip months (even if there's not much news)

2. **Be Honest**
   - Share both good and bad news
   - Don't sugarcoat challenges
   - Investors appreciate transparency and want to help

3. **Be Specific**
   - Use numbers, not vague language
   - "Grew 30%" not "growing fast"
   - "Closed 15 customers" not "lots of new customers"

4. **Show Momentum**
   - Month-over-month comparisons
   - Trend lines (improving or declining)
   - Progress toward goals

5. **Make Actionable Asks**
   - Be specific about how investors can help
   - Make it easy for them to respond
   - Follow up if they offer help

6. **Keep It Scannable**
   - Use headers, bullets, tables
   - Bold key numbers and names
   - TL;DR at the top

### Don'ts

1. **Don't Skip Bad Months**
   - Investors notice silence
   - Bad news gets worse if you wait
   - Show you're addressing issues proactively

2. **Don't Be Vague**
   - "Doing well" tells investors nothing
   - Always quantify when possible

3. **Don't Make It Too Long**
   - Keep to 5-10 minute read
   - Link to deeper materials if needed
   - Investors are busy

4. **Don't Focus Only on Product**
   - Investors care about metrics and traction
   - Product updates are important but secondary to business metrics

5. **Don't Forget to Ask for Help**
   - Investors want to add value
   - Specific asks get responses
   - Generic "let me know if you can help" gets ignored

## Cadence & Timing

### Monthly Updates

**When:** First week of each month (e.g., first Monday or first business day)

**Why:** 
- Allows time to close books and calculate metrics
- Creates consistent expectation
- Fresh data (not 3 weeks stale)

### Quarterly Deep Dives (Optional)

**When:** End of each quarter (Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct)

**Format:** Video call or in-person if possible

**Purpose:**
- Deeper discussion than email allows
- Get strategic advice from investors
- Build stronger relationships

### Annual Meeting (Optional but Recommended)

**When:** End of year or start of new year

**Format:** In-person or video call with all investors

**Purpose:**
- Year in review
- Strategic planning for next year
- Thank investors for support
