# Weekly Metrics Review Template

> A structured template for weekly leadership team metrics reviews - the heartbeat ritual that keeps your team aligned, accountable, and moving fast.

---

## Overview

Weekly metrics reviews are the most important ritual for maintaining momentum and catching issues early. This document provides:
- Meeting structure and agenda
- Metrics to review each week
- How to identify and address problems
- Action item tracking framework

**Related documents:**
- `kpi-framework.md` - Complete metric definitions
- `dashboard-mockup.md` - Dashboard views for weekly review
- `north-star-metric.md` - Your primary success metric
- `investor-update.md` - Monthly external reporting
- `../03-product/success-metrics.md` - Product-specific metrics
- `../04-finance/unit-economics.md` - Unit economics deep dive

**Meeting Details:**
- **Frequency:** Every Monday, 9:00 AM, 30 minutes (never skip, never reschedule)
- **Attendees:** CEO, CFO, CRO, VP Sales, VP Marketing, Head of Product, Head of CS
- **Location:** Conference room or video call
- **Owner:** CEO (or COO if applicable)

## Why Weekly Reviews Matter

### The Cadence Principle

**Monthly is too slow:**
- Problems compound for 4 weeks before you notice
- Lost opportunities to course-correct
- Team loses sense of urgency

**Daily is too fast:**
- Not enough time to see trends
- Noisy data (random fluctuations)
- Team spends all time in meetings

**Weekly is the sweet spot:**
- Enough data to see trends
- Fast enough to catch problems early
- Creates healthy rhythm and accountability

### Benefits of Weekly Reviews

1. **Early Warning System** - Catch problems in week 1, not week 4
2. **Accountability** - Team knows metrics will be reviewed weekly
3. **Alignment** - Everyone sees the same numbers and priorities
4. **Learning** - Regular retros on what's working, what's not
5. **Momentum** - Celebrate wins weekly, not just quarterly

## Meeting Structure (30 Minutes)

### Agenda

```
0:00 - 0:05 | Check-in & Wins
0:05 - 0:15 | Metrics Review
0:15 - 0:25 | Deep Dive on One Topic
0:25 - 0:30 | Action Items & Priorities
```

**Rules:**
- Start and end on time (respect everyone's calendars)
- No laptops/phones (except to pull up dashboards)
- Be honest and direct (no sugarcoating)
- Focus on solutions, not blame
- End with clear action items and owners

## Meeting Hygiene

### Do's

1. **Start and End on Time**
   - Respect everyone's calendars
   - If you need more time, schedule a separate deep dive

2. **Come Prepared**
   - Review dashboard before meeting
   - Know your numbers
   - Have context for changes

3. **Be Data-Driven**
   - Use numbers, not opinions
   - "Activation rate dropped 5%" not "onboarding feels broken"

4. **Focus on Solutions**
   - Don't dwell on problems without action plans
   - "What are we going to do about it?"

5. **Be Honest**
   - No sugarcoating
   - Call out issues early
   - Team can't fix what they don't know about

### Don'ts

1. **Don't Skip**
   - Consistency is key
   - Even if there's "not much to report"
   - Absence sends wrong message

2. **Don't Go Over Time**
   - Scope creep kills the ritual
   - Schedule separate meetings for deep dives

3. **Don't Blame**
   - Focus on the problem, not the person
   - "Churn is up, what can we do?" not "Why did you let churn increase?"

4. **Don't Get Sidetracked**
   - Stay on agenda
   - Park tangential discussions ("let's take this offline")

5. **Don't Accept Vague Action Items**
   - "Improve activation" is not an action item
   - "Reduce Step 3 drop-off from 30% to 20% by Friday" is

## Red Flags & Escalations

### When to Escalate

If any of these happen, escalate to full exec team immediately (don't wait for weekly meeting):

**Revenue Red Flags:**
- MRR growth <5% MoM for 2 consecutive weeks
- Large customer churn (>$10K MRR)
- Miss weekly new customer target by >30%

**Product Red Flags:**
- Activation rate drops >10% week-over-week
- Platform uptime <95% in any day
- Critical bug impacting >10% of customers

**Operational Red Flags:**
- Runway drops below 12 months
- Key executive departure
- Major competitive threat (acquisition, funding, product launch)

**Customer Red Flags:**
- Churn spike (>2x normal weekly churn)
- NPS drops below 40
- Multiple customers citing same issue

## Metrics Trends to Watch

### Good Trends (Reinforce)

- ✅ Activation rate improving week-over-week
- ✅ Churn declining consistently
- ✅ WAU growing faster than signups (retention improving)
- ✅ CAC declining (efficiency gains)
- ✅ ARPA increasing (expansion working)
- ✅ Sales cycle shortening
- ✅ Win rate improving

**Action:** Double down on what's working. Ask "Why is this working? How can we do more of it?"

---

### Bad Trends (Address Immediately)

- 🚨 Activation rate declining (onboarding broken)
- 🚨 Churn increasing (product, CS, or product-market fit issue)
- 🚨 WAU flat while signups grow (activation or retention problem)
- 🚨 CAC increasing (marketing inefficiency or wrong audience)
- 🚨 Sales cycle lengthening (sales process, product, or market issue)
- 🚨 Win rate declining (competition or weak positioning)

**Action:** Root cause analysis. What changed? Why? What experiments can we run this week to fix it?

## Quarterly Retrospective

**Every 13 weeks, do a meta-review of the weekly review process itself.**

### Questions to Ask

1. **Is this meeting still valuable?**
   - What's working well?
   - What's not working?
   - Should we change format, timing, or attendees?

2. **Are we tracking the right metrics?**
   - Any metrics we should add?
   - Any metrics we should remove (not actionable)?
   - Are targets still appropriate?

3. **Are we taking action?**
   - What % of action items get completed?
   - If <80%, why? (Too many? Unclear owners? Not important enough?)

4. **Are we escalating appropriately?**
   - Did we catch issues early enough?
   - Any surprises that should have been flagged earlier?

5. **How can we improve?**
   - Feedback from team?
   - Changes to format or agenda?
